Well here in New Zealand is one place where we don't get it.

What you need to build your own DGPS base station (for post-processing):
 - an extra Garmin
 - software to save the raw Garmin protocol
 - a datalogger/laptop + car battery
 - a known survey benchmark pin where you can leave the recording gear
   or a friendly surveyor who'll make you one where it's convinent
 - a datalogger/laptop to record the raw stream from the moving receiver
 - software to process everything once you get home

What you need to build your own WAAS:
 - an atomic clock about the size of a VW bug
 - spooky RF antenna know-how
 - a rocket to put it into geosynchronous orbit
 - some sort of personal NORAD communications center to look after it
 - ...

;-)

[the above is to encourage folks to try, and to explain why it is not
unusual for far-off places to have DGPS coverage]
