For RS232 anything above three volts is a "space" or "0" and anything
below -3V is a "mark" or a "1".  This is polar inverse of TTL.

I haven't looked at the GPS code for yonks, but I am wondering if the code
was written to accomodate TTL inputs on RS232 ports as the other day I had
a TTL input on DTS and was getting the 0-1-0 PPS transitions marked in the
GPSD debug output.

A while ago a built a system where I used a MAX232 TTL to RS232 converter
changing the pulse (mark) to occur as a negative voltage transition from
the quiescent positive voltage in contrast to a positive pulse using
stright TTL.

GPSD would only detect the 0-1-0 transtions properly when I added a NOT
gate between the RS232 chip and the PPS signal essentially forcing the
"mark" back to being a positive voltage excursion on the DTS pin.

So beware, if you use and RS232 level coverter on the PPS signal bear in
mind you may also need an inverter to sort out any further PPS signal
problems.
