Chris Kuethe <chris.kuethe at gmail.com>:
> On Sat, May 24, 2008 at 10:37 AM, Paul Fox <pgf at foxharp.boston.ma.us> wrote:
> >                                                    how much would giving
> > the gps bad data (e.g., time off by a few seconds) hurt?  and
> > which of these would give the biggest bang for the buck.
> 
> Could hurt a lot... like multiple tens of minutes if the firmware is
> really dumb.
> 
> Some receivers may keep trying with bad data indefinitely until you
> command them to do an auto-locate (cold start).

Chris is right.  I looked into this issue and concluded that the net
gain from supporting some kind of initialization poke was probably
negative -- that is, it would probably hurt more often than help (and,
worse, there is no good way to predict when it would help).  That's
why gpsd has no such feature.
