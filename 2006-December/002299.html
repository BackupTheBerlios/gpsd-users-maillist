> Sorry, what is DR? (too tired to look at the code now).

Dead Reckoning. Automotive-integrated sets (for example) may take
odometer ticks off the drivetrain, and gyro/accelerometer outputs from
the ABS and mash those into the navigation solution when the GPS
signal is degraded/unavailable.

My laptop (Thinkpad T42) has accelerometers onboard. I did up a kernel
patch to make them output at 100Hz and calibrated the output, so I
have some fairly entertaining logs from my last time out at the
racetrack. I was foolish and didn't put my SiRF into binary mode
before I started logging, so I really only have graphs of fairly well
correlated position, speed, acceleration and time, but not closely
linked enough to postprocess... at least, i don't think they're good
enough.

> Off the top of my head, I can't think of anything else other than the

Hm. That'll require a different message format. For now, the O message
(7DOF PVT solution with 95% confidence intervals) is what we've got.
It's a good format. We're not dismissing this extra RTK metadata
outright, but I don't think it has a place in the standard PVT report.

Stick around though - you can help us with the Next Generation
Protocol. As an actual user of RTK gear, you can tell us whether or
not a message format contains enough useful information.
