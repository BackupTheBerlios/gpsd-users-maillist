On Mon, 4 Dec 2006, Greg Troxel wrote:

>   The gpsd garmin binary ONLY works over USB at this time.   Porting it
>   to serial gives you no useful advantage at this time.
>
> I'm still getting up to speed, so dropping the list for what's
> probably a stupid question.  I have a GPS V, which is serial only,
> although I now hook it up via USB sometimes.

By USB I mean the USB chip is IN the Garmin, not merely a USB<-> serial
dongle between the host and the Garmin.

So, for example, the Garmin GPS-18/USB speaks ONLY Garmin USB binary
ad the Garmin GPS-18/LVC speaks either NMEA or Garmin Serial Binary.

> I believe it speaks garmin binary.

Dunno.  You could look it up on the Garmin web site.  Or just try to get
garminctl to force garmin mode on your unit.

> Are there
> really two binary protocols,

Yes.

> and the new one is only on newer receivers?

No.
>
One Garmin binary is on Garmin with an RS-232 or other serial type (LVC)
connector.

The other Garmin binary is on the Garmins with builtin USB support.
