looking at that ublox page, i can see why someone might think it's for
automotive use: they've removed the flash memory, they're running
high-sensitivity firmware, it's fairly energy-efficient and it
supports >1Hz fixes.

The NMEA problem is that no single NMEA sentence contains a full PVT
solution. To get a complete state measurement you need to reassemble
all the sentences into a coherent view, and that adds latency. By
sending "J=1" to gpsd, you can cause gpsd to try reassemble a complete
fix for you, at the cost of some additional delay and the risk of
using stale, invalid data.
