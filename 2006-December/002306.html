On 12/4/06, Eric S. Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com> wrote:
> However, one of the ways we may end up extending O is by adding
> optional private-use fields to the end of the report.  These would be
> used for numeric metadata that is closely coupled to a PVT report --
> for example, water depth or temperature.
>
> Those would differ from the separate GPSD-NG queries, which would be
> for things like satellite ephemeris data *not* closely associated
> with an individual PVT report.

Gut feeling says that's not the right approach, hanging metadata off the end...

Protocol 3 is pretty complete for what it's supposed to do... supply
PVT[1], either in simple question-and-answer format, or as a full
report. It feels very nearly permanently frozen (modulo critical
bugfixes).

Any extensions are probably best added to protocol 4, which we get to
design from scratch with a view to crazy, unimaginable future uses of
unified ... environmental and dynamics information. Extensions are
needed when gpsd is doing more than the simple job it was first
intended to do - don't mess with a good thing.

[1] PVT != polarization of ambient light, volume of the stereo and
temperature of beer...
