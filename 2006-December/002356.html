No, actually, it doesn't.  And it ties in nicely to some of the
discussion of GPSD-NG.  We start having a significant
namespace-management problem, but maybe that was inevitable anyway.
 
>    Hmm... As I described it above, the client could define its reports
> any way it likes, so it could lump every bit of info it needs in a
> single message.  Wouldn't that solve the jitter problem? 

Only if there is genuinely only one message per cycle.

>                                                          Additionally,
> if you trigger the report "on change", defining "change" as a variation
> on *any* of the data contained in the report (perhaps other than time),
> then you would also solve the latency problem, albeit at the expense of

Oh, boy, does that open a can of worms.  Consider how roundoff errors 
could make the presence of a report effectively random...

I'm not shooting down your ideas. I'm pointing out that they seem to me 
a bit half-baked and need more time in the oven.  

For the near future I want to concentrate on merging the new drivers and
cleaning up the known problems in the regression tests.  I think we should
be looking at solving those problems and issuing a 2.35 before diving into
a big redesign,
