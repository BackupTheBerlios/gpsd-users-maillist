> It is,. The packet-sniffer is a gpsd module that automatically
> recognizes incoming GPS protocol streams.  Recognizer logic for each
> protocol has to be merged into the sniffer's state machine.

It's a both senses.

The packet sniffer Ali mentions is what Andreas and I use to pull raw
bytes off the wire to generate test/development loads. This is key to
developing support for new protocols when the data on the wire does
not match the documented behaviour.

The packet sniffer Eric mentions is the part of gpsd that looks at the
wire protocol, detects a packet (which necessarily means identifying
what sort of packet it is) and passes it off to that protocol's
decoder for further processing.

Sometimes your sniffer is a simple logger, other times it's a
full-blown protocol dissector.
