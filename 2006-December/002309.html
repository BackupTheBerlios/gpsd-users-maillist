Chris Kuethe <chris.kuethe at gmail.com>:
> On 12/4/06, Eric S. Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com> wrote:
> >There are certainly reasons to be wary of private-use fields.  But I
> >don't see how else we can tie metadata to individual PVT reports without
> >creating yet another stupid jitter-vs.latency fight.  The last thing
> >we want to do is recreate the horror that is NMEA altitude reporting
> >at the application level.
> 
> I'm not opposed to private fields, I just don't want to bloat protocol
> 3. It's simple, elegant and informative.
> 
> If we need a richer report format, I'm all for it, but let's design
> something new - maybe take "H" and use that as the
> O-message-plus-a-state-of-the-union-speech message.

I'm at a loss to see what problem this would solve.

Suppose we do as you suggest.  OK, there's an H message. It duplicates
O, plus it has private fields.  

The duplicative information is unpleasant to start with.  It raises
a couple of issues.  Like, do H commands get streamed to clients in
watcher mode?  And if your answer is "yes", then why are we bothering
with the O message at all?

There's nothing sacred about the field count of the O message.  I've
increased it at least three times since I originally invented watcher mode.
The libgps parser ignores 'extra' fields (that is, more than it was designed
to handle) deliberately, so older libgps versions won't get confused
by newer daemons.
