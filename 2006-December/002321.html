Greg Troxel <gdt at ir.bbn.com>:
> Louis Mamakos <louie at transsys.com> writes:
> 
> > On Sat, Dec 09, 2006 at 08:02:42PM +1300, Hamish wrote:
> >> 
> >> Universal %.9f for degrees and %.3f for meters? ie aim for 1mm (better
> >> than horiz RTK).
> >
> > Isn't usually the case in an engineering discipline to display/write
> > only as many digits as there is precision in the underlying data?
> 
> Certainly, when communicating among engineers.  Here, we have a
> protocol which uses ASCII, and is semantically just sending something
> that is at heart a floating-point representation.  There are
> associated accuracy representations.  I don't think it's appropriate
> to lose precision arbitrarily in intermediate computational steps.
> 
> The real problem is that we have several basic choices:
> 
>   pick a resolution that's "always" high enough, and expect the user
>   to be clueful enough to look at EPE or UERE and DOP.
> 
>   pick a resolution that's sometimes too low, and lose accuracy from
>   rounding in some cases.
> 
>   have the protocol dynamically change resolution based on EPE.  This
>   seems too complicated and unnecessary.
> 
> Of these, only the first seems reasonable.  Imagine if NTP used ASN.1
> to have a variable number of bits after the radix point, instead of
> always way too many :-)

Greg's analysis is correct, or at least matches mine.  The first alternative
seems to me the least of evils.
