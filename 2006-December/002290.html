Greetings gents,

at the present moment, gpsd outputs lat and lon information only to six
decimal places. For a mid-latitude position that equals to about 10cm
resolution.

Since we might be using (and indeed I am) DGPS or RTK equipment which offers
centimetre precision or less, I believe it would be a good idea to consider
increasing the resolution of the output---9 decimal places should do it.
Similarly for the heights in the W stream, which come at two decimal places,
whereas A returns three, which is in my opinion a better choice.

This change appears to me trivial to implement and shouldn't (right!) break
any well-behaved clients. Any thoughts?

Regards,
Diego Berge.
