Carl Brown <cbsled at verizon.net>:
> One general use case to consider in this design phase is the marine use of an 
> on-board computer. The input to gpsd will almost certainly be NMEA, either 
> 0183 or the new 2000 which allows all instrumentation to operate on a single 
> com circuit. 
> 
> In this case, gpsd would need to read and interpret the complete NMEA data 
> stream, which could include engine oil pressure, fuel levels and anything 
> else for which you can build a sensor, including the temperature of the beer 
> in the fridge, scrambled in with PVT and PVT-related data such as depth, 
> rudder position, or apparent wind speed and direction. 
> 
> In this environment, gpsd would be expected to supply all of the available 
> data to applications, whatever that data might be. 

This is the kind of multi-source environment we have GPSD-NG in mind for.
