Yo Balazs!

On Fri, 12 Dec 2008, Balazs Csak wrote:

> It seems that the whole stuff runs without problem. Gpsd reads the NMEA
> sentences, the PPS signal, and ntp also find and use these.

Excellent.

> The problem is that
> finally there is always a delay in our clock compared to other time
> servers by ntpdate. The length of the delay is always exactly the
> same as the length of PPS > pulse, independently of the hardware
> configuration (1, 2 or 3).

Maybe you are triggering on the wrong edge of the pulse?

You can invert the pulse in hardware, invert in gpsd software
or just add a fudge value in ntp.conf to correct for the fixed error.

Here is my fudge:

fudge 127.127.28.1 time1 0.0010 refid GPS1
