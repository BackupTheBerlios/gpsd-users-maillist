On Wed, 3 Dec 2008, Robert Cunningham wrote:
> I'm invoking gpsd as follows:
>   /usr/sbin/gpsd -n /dev/ttyUSB0 &

Just to echo what Chris said, something like

strace /usr/sbin/gpsd -n /dev/ttyUSB0 2>gpsd-syscalls.txt

will put the system calls that gpsd is making into gpsd-syscalls.txt.

If gpsd isn't doing it, it might be that your USB-to-serial driver
(assuming that's what you use) is glomping on to the parallel port
(or at least its I/O range) for some reason.  If you have a
USB-to-serial converter that uses a different chip or driver, you might
change to that converter and see if it makes any difference.
Alternatively, with gpsd not running, start up minicom or equal and
point it at /dev/ttyUSB0; at the right bit rate you should see NMEA
sentences or binary soup coming from your GPS.  With minicom running,
try using the parallel port; if you are still blocked from using the
parallel port, the problem is at a layer below gpsd.

I hope this helps!
