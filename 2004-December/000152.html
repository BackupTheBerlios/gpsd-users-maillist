> And is there any way to gpsd say something like this:
> When there you lost the gps-signals to the satellits, calculate the 
> current position each second with the last valid position, the heading
> and the speed?

This sort of automatic switch to dead-reckoning once GPS is lost is what
lead to the Royal Majesty (a 568-foot cruise ship) grounding itself on
the Nantucket Shoals a few years ago. Third mate on watch on the night 
shift didn't know what the flashing red light meant and wasn't watching
the sounder.

see  http://www.capecodonline.com/special/andreadoria/capewrecks22.htm


Not recommended. Bad info is worse than no info.
