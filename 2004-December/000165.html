Once upon a time, one arm of the US military scrambled the civilian
version of the GPS signal to hinder its use by ne'er do wells.
Another arm of the military (coast guard) found this to be annoying, so
set up a system of surveyed ground stations and broadcast via VHF radio
corrections which when merged with the GPS signals could get you from
100 foot to 10 foot accuracy (DGPS) which is handy for getting ships
into channels in zero-viz.. The FAA decided the would be cool for
airplanes as well but because of lack of DGPS coverage away from the
coasts they decided to put up their own correction satellite (WAAS).
Then in May 2000 Pres. Clinton decided that the whole scramble-then-
unscramble thing was stupid & holding back emergency service uses (eg
which side of the interstate to send the fire truck to) so he did away
with the introduced scrambling (SA) on the civilian frequency.

[or something like that]

Note DGPS (as multi-band military GPS and WAAS) will do some corrections
for ionospheric errors, so DGPS is still better than unscrambled-GPS.
