On Wed, 13 Apr 2011, Mihail Popa wrote:

> I configured gpsd 2.92 on my machine (OpenSUSE 11.3 x64) to communicate with
> a GPS module connected on /dev/ttyS0.

2.92 is a bit old.  But might work OK for you.

> gpsd: PPS pps-detect (DCD) on /dev/ttyS0 changed to 0
> gpsd: PPS cycle: 999509, duration: 899607 @ 1302708420.790740
> gpsd: PPS 1Hz leading edge
> gpsd: PPS ntpshm_pps 1302708426.000 @ 1302708420.790740, preci 3

This is the leading edge and passed to ntpd just fine.

> gpsd: PPS pps-detect (DCD) on /dev/ttyS0 changed to 1
> gpsd: PPS cycle: 999509, duration: 99902 @ 1302708420.890642
> gpsd: PPS 1Hz trailing edge
> gpsd: PPS pulse rejected*

This is the trailing edge and has no use.

> It's "PPS pulse rejected" that actually scares me... Is it a bad thing?

Nope.  What you see is normal debug info.  Time for you to start looking
at your ntpd.
