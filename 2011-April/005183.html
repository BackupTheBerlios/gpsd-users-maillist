tz <thomas at mich.com>:
> I have three things, mostly my tasks:
> 
> 1. NO_FLOAT

That's a big one. We'll probably go through a couple of release cycles
shaking it down.

Which reminds me.  We had another ARM guy show up on IRC reporting
a horrible performance problem on an arm9.  He had gpsd eating half
the processor.

After some profiling and microtuning, it looked like the remaining problem 
was expensive float instructions.  I thought of you, of course. But!
It turned out he had unintentionally disabled an instruction-caching feature.
When he undid that, gpsd's processor usage dropped down to accasional 1% 
spikes but mostly too low to make it out of statistical noise.

Accordingly, I think you need to check your assumption that float
instructions are a serious performance problem more carefully. Because
now I've actually seen ARM profile results that looked that way but
were masking a different problem.

Not that I'm not saying this to avoid doing NOFLOAT; I think it will
be a good idea on general principles. But we should be proceeding on
a basis of reality.  If there's a misdiagnosis here, now is the time
to figure that out.
