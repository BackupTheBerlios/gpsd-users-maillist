On Mon, Apr 18, 2011 at 09:48:34AM +0200, Roger Oberholtzer wrote:
R> I am considering if I want to use GPSD to maintain accurate time in a
R> measurement system that we build. The relevant system characteristics
R> are that:
R> 
R>  * the system is in a vehicle that travels on roads.
R> 
R>  * the computer will not be connected to the internet.
R> 
R>  * the vehicle may occasionally be located in a building, and then
R> unable to access the sky. This is especially true when the system is
R> initially powered on.
R> 
R>  * being in the real world, the satellite signals may degrade from time
R> to time. For example, when driving on a road bound by tall trees, or in
R> urban canyons. We do have an INS that helps with our locations during
R> these times. But I do not know how time is effected.
R> 
R>  * the GPS receivers are rather high quality survey-grade devices from
R> Trimble. The PPS signal is available.
R> 
R> I guess I am trying to figure out the dynamics of such a system. For
R> example, if the system is powered on when there are no satellites
R> available, how long after they become available would one expect that
R> the system time may be correct? How long might one be without GPS
R> signals before time is effected? What other questions should I be asking
R> here?
R> 
R> I am sure there are more important questions I should be asking. I
R> realize that these are very system-dependent questions, and so any
R> answers may not apply in my case. I am really just curious if there are
R> any others here who are using gpsd for a similar use. If so, what sort
R> of issues have they encountered, and have they found solutions.

I am using GPSD in car. I'm not interested in nano-second precision, so
I have USB receiver, and I use NTP shared memory segment for
synchronisation.

Answer to all your "how long ...?" questions is "time varies depending
on conditions". :) However, if car is outside of building it usually
takes less than minute.
