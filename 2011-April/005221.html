2011/4/18 Roger Oberholtzer <roger.oberholtzer at ramboll.se>:
> I am considering if I want to use GPSD to maintain accurate time in a
> measurement system that we build. The relevant system characteristics
> are that:
>
>  * the system is in a vehicle that travels on roads.
>
>  * the computer will not be connected to the internet.
>
>  * the vehicle may occasionally be located in a building, and then
> unable to access the sky. This is especially true when the system is
> initially powered on.
>
>  * being in the real world, the satellite signals may degrade from time
> to time. For example, when driving on a road bound by tall trees, or in
> urban canyons. We do have an INS that helps with our locations during
> these times. But I do not know how time is effected.
>
>  * the GPS receivers are rather high quality survey-grade devices from
> Trimble. The PPS signal is available.
>
> I guess I am trying to figure out the dynamics of such a system. For
> example, if the system is powered on when there are no satellites
> available, how long after they become available would one expect that
> the system time may be correct? How long might one be without GPS
> signals before time is effected? What other questions should I be asking
> here?
>
> I am sure there are more important questions I should be asking. I
> realize that these are very system-dependent questions, and so any
> answers may not apply in my case. I am really just curious if there are
> any others here who are using gpsd for a similar use. If so, what sort
> of issues have they encountered, and have they found solutions.

Just to make sure you are aware of it, for this type of application
gpsd + chrony seems to be a preferred over ntpd.  Chrony was build
with intermittent timesource availably in mind.  This is from multiple email
 list threads which have mitigated towards this solution in the past.

http://chrony.tuxfamily.org/
