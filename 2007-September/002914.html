Yo Michael!

On Fri, 28 Sep 2007, Michael St. Laurent wrote:

> The PPS is at TTL level but my hardware seems able to deal with that as
> according to the gpsd logs it's sensing the voltage transition.

Also be sure you have the proper polarity.

> ... it brings the PPS output high for about 500 ms.

That is a square wave, not a pulse.

>  However
> it's complaining that the pulse is too short and not providing PPS info
> to NTP through the shm interface.

And it is too short.  The PPS is a PULSE Per Second, not a square wave.

With a PULSE we can know which edge to use.  With a square wave it is
hard to know.  Some GPS have a command to adjust the duty cycle of
the pulse.  Is that an option for you?  If not we could help you
patch out the check, but the check is usefull in catching real world
noise problems so I hate to remove it entirely.
