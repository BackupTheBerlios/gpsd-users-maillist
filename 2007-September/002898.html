On 9/14/07, Hamish <hamish_nospam at yahoo.com> wrote:
> does anyone know the correct syntax to use for that on MacOSX? (or BSD?)

"that depends."  on bsd-ish unix-like operating systems, you should
use the cu devices, rather than tty devices, if available.

> Obviously the port name is different; for me gpsd works fine on OSX but for
> testing I can't cat the device as I don't know how to set the port speed to
> 4800. I just get wrong port speed garbage printed to the terminal when I try.

on my openbsd machines, i use "/dev/cuaU0"
on my friend's mac mini, i use "/dev/cu.usbserial"

device node to physical device mapping is very os specific. also, this
is why i like minicom - i point it at a port, and with a couple of
keystrokes i can change port settings on the fly until i find
something that works.
