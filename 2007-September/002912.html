I'm working with the GPSClock 200 unit which uses a Furuno Electric
GH-79L4-N engine.

Here is what the web page says about the time signaling:

"The GPSClock 200 has an RS-232 output that provides NMEA time codes and
a PPS output signal. About a half-second before, it outputs the time of
the next PPS pulse in either GPRMC or GPZDA format. Within one
microsecond of the beginning of the UTC second, it brings the PPS output
high for about 500 ms."

The PPS is at TTL level but my hardware seems able to deal with that as
according to the gpsd logs it's sensing the voltage transition.  However
it's complaining that the pulse is too short and not providing PPS info
to NTP through the shm interface.

Sep 27 12:41:15 hcnas gpsd[1773]: gpsd: pps-detect (DCD) on /dev/ttyS0
changed t
o 0
Sep 27 12:41:15 hcnas gpsd[1773]: gpsd: PPS pulse rejected too short.
cycle: 100
0009, duration: 499997
Sep 27 12:41:15 hcnas gpsd[1773]: gpsd: <= GPS:
$GPRMC,194115,A,3351.7903,N,1174
9.5205,W,000.0,333.7,270907,013.3,E,A*0F^M
Sep 27 12:41:16 hcnas gpsd[1773]: gpsd: pps-detect (DCD) on /dev/ttyS0
changed t
o 1
Sep 27 12:41:16 hcnas gpsd[1773]: gpsd: PPS pulse rejected too short.
cycle: 100
0033, duration: 500036

So I'm thinking that it's either that the pulse is too long or because
the NMEA is 1/2 sec *before* the second mark.

Comments?
