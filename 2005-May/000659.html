The bug queue has cleared with amazing speed since 2.24 was released 
just a few days ago.  Special thanks to Lonnie Mendez who helped nail
the nasty bug in GPGSV parsing that was occasionally clobbering the 
device name.

Here's what the release notes would look like if I shipped now:

- Various signedness and scaling fixes and an OpenBSD port patch for the 
  Zodiac driver. Command-line arguments to gpsd are now treated as a default 
  device list; -f is still supported but deprecated.  sirfmon now tries not
  changing the line speed first, so it syncs up much faster.  Prevent a 
  potential buffer overrun in the client library.  PPS-thread support is now
  on by default.  Lots of documentation improvements. D-BUS broadcast support 
  by Amaury Jacquot.  Added Alfredo Pironti's thread-callback and C++
  support.

Anybody have any urgent issues in the way of a 2.25 release?  If not,
I'll ship late tonight or early tomorrow.

Once again I must say that even with my long experience of open-source 
development I am quite impressed by the quality of the work this group
is turning in.  Take a bow, everybody!
