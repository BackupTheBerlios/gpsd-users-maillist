Yo Eric!

On Fri, 20 May 2005, Eric S. Raymond wrote:

> I know what the fix for the time_t problem is.  64-bit hardware --
> which I'm sure will be nigh-universal in fifteen years, let alone
> thirty.  Heck, I'm already on an AMD-64 box.  A 64-bit time_t won't
> wrap around for approximately five hundred eighty five billion years.

Knowing and implementing are not the same thing....  Wanna bet it is
not as simple as changing time_t?  Either way it is a big guess.

> Making a 21st-century assumption would only help gpsd in a rather unusual
> case.  The GPS has to be NMEA-only (e.g. not SiRF, not Garmin, not
> Zodiac).  It has to not issue ZDA (Motorola and FV-18 chips issue ZDA).
> *And* your system clock has to be so fubar that it's indicating the
> wrong century.
>

> Does this seem likely?

Yep, any time my CMOS battery goes dead or gets reset, the PC comes up Dec
31, 1969.  My EC-10X has no idea of the century.  So yes it is very
conceiveable to happen.  And it seems to help out the guy that requested
the feature.

Consider this, how likely is it that time will go backwards to 19xx?  How
likely is it that folks will use NMEA in 2100?

That said, it is merely academic to me...
