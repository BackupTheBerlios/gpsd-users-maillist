After considering the use cases carefully, I've decided that it really
is kind of bogus for a daemon that reads from GPSes to rely on the
local system clock for anything.  Instead, if it hasn't been handed a
valid century part by GPS output, it assumes it's running in the 21st
century.

Accordingly, gpsd no longer has to rely on the system clock under any
circumstances. This will make no difference to SiRF, Garmin, or Zodiac
users, as those binary protocols deliver full timestamps.  Nor will it
matter if your NMEA-only device delivers GPZDA, which includes a
4-digit year.

Nor will it matter until the end of 2099.  As Gary Miller pointed out
on the cev list, the 2038 wraparound of 32-bit wraparound of the Unix
time_t is going to force us all to recompile for 64 bits sooner than
that.
