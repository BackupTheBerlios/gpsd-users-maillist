There are two things that can seriously get in your way with GPS:
interface and protocol. In your case, you're using bluetooth, so you
won't have a problem with some new-and-unsupported usb-serial chip
getting in your way. You might have a problem with the Serial Port
Profile on bluetooth if you attempt to adjust the device settings, but
if you're just expecting to turn it on and have NMEA coming out, then
you're in good shape.

The second issue is protocol: we are able to process UBX, SiRF, iTalk,
TSIP, Garmin, Evermore, Navcom, Zodiac... They're all proprietary
protocols, and we also process the industry-standard NMEA protocol,
which is what the BTG-7000 outputs by default.

Basically, we don't have the information or motivation at this time to
support any nifty features that may be provided by the Nemerix or Sony
chips. That being said, as long as they output generic NMEA, they will
work with gpsd.

Overall, as a magic black box you should be OK. The BTG-7000 outputs
NMEA over the serial port profile. That's not the optimal
configuration, but it's enough to give you a straight answer to "where
am i?"

Getting up on my soapbox for a moment: open documentation is important
to all of you. It means we can write and maintain drivers, and you can
validate our work. If you look in the list archives you'll see an
instance of me writing something to the hardware... backwards, and it
was caught by one of our sharp-eyed users. Documentation helps you get
more out of the hardware you bought, and helps maintain its
usefulness. Support vendors who provide documentation to the open
source community.
