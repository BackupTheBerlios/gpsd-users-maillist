> Nemerix and Sony are both supported insofar as we can parse their
> NMEA. They're not quite as supported as something like SiRF because we
> don't have any sample hardware and full protocol docs. It's probably
> good that we don't have specific protocol support for these two yet
> because speed/protocol changes on bluetooth tend to be somewhat
> problematic.
> 
> Sony's chip doesn't look like it does anything fancy to make binary
> support worthwhile. Or maybe it's just their front end that is lame;
> iSuite from Fastrax (makers of iTrax) can use the Sony chip set and
> get lots of interesting data out of it. Nemerix is being uncooperative
> about releasing the protocol documentation. If you'd like to help with
> getting Nemerix more supported, you can email Lewis.Boore at nemerix.com
> and try explain how it'll help you to have the protocol specification
> open.
> 
> Doc *and* hardware are important for ongoing device support.
