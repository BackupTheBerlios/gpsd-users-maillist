Zanardi <gasperotti at supereva.it>:
> Version 2.3 or 3.1 (there are 4 mountpoint/stream and I can select the
> version that I prefer).
> 
> I need to integrate this decoder in my project (my project is write in C and
> based on NtripLinuxClient), is difficult in your opinion integrate the RTCM
> decoder?

Presently we only support 2.1, and the code is not very well tested yet.
(we do have one 2.1 load for it in the test subdirectory).  It is
possible that skew between 2.1 and 2.3 might be your problem.

Unfortunately, I fear you'd find pulling the 2.1 decoder out of gpsd quite
difficult. The problem is that the decoder logic is deeply tangled up with
gpsd's packet recognizer, the logic that tries to automatically recognize 
GPS or RTCM or NTRIP report packets.

On the other hand, I do *not* recommend trying to write your own decoder.
RTCM 3.x is not too bad, but 2.1 is the flat-out *ugliest* bitstream protocol
I've ever seen. It is poorly documented and extremely difficult to get right.

Perhaps your project could use GPSD's client library to communicate with a GPSD
instance collecting RTCM data for you?  That's what I'd do in your shoes.

I'd be happy to cooperate you on improving gpsd's RTCM support.
