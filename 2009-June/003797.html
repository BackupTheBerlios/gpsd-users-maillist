you are assuming that the pinnout would be the same. There's a good chance
that it isn't. PS/2 is just the shape of the connector, not how it is
wired up or what protocol it uses. A small round connector is nice if you
need to drill just big enough O.D. holes through walls to pass the wire
through.

e.g. the PS/2 pinnout listed here (for another GPS) has the following in
big red letters next to the diagram:

http://www.semsons.com/glmrbuwagpsp.html

"MR-350P uses a propriatary pin-out on the PS2, which cannot be connected
directly to any laptop or PC without the use of a cable set (USB or RS232
+ PS2 for power)"

You you knew the wiring for your model and were up for that sort of
challenge there is a good chance you could solder up the cable yourself.

As it is I expect it is not talking because the transmit/receive lines
are not in the correct holes.


> PS/ important to say: I bought this on Ebay, and the plastic case of the

At this point I'd be returning the thing as defective and rating the
vendor as appropriate.

If it is missing functional parts or not (USB/serial adapter cable) as
well would depend in the manner in which it was advertised or represented.
