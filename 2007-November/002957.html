Yo Askar!

On Thu, 8 Nov 2007, Askar Ibragimov wrote:

> actually, I am just a bit curious - if we got two sources of time, GPS
> (just) and GPS+PPS, what is wrong in accepting PPS pulse even it is
> far from second boundary?

Because the PPS does not tell you WHICH second it is.  Since your
base clock is wandering more than a second the system could just as
soon lock to the wrong second.

You should be able to get stable within a few hundred mS using just the
NMEA time from the GPS over 9600.  Your 38,400 should be able to do
better than that.  Do not worry about the precise things until you can
get the gross things fixed first.  Fix your basic time problem first.
