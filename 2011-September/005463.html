You might try using gcc:

--------------------------------------------------
-*- mode: compilation; default-directory: "~/projects/gpsmm/" -*-
Compilation started at Sun Sep  4 08:52:00

gcc gpsmm.cpp
gpsmm.cpp: In function ‘int main()’:
gpsmm.cpp:11: error: no matching function for call to ‘gpsmm::gpsmm()’
/usr/local/include/libgpsmm.h:21: note: candidates are:
gpsmm::gpsmm(const char*, const
char*) /usr/local/include/libgpsmm.h:15: note:
gpsmm::gpsmm(const gpsmm&) gpsmm.cpp:13: error: invalid use of ‘class 
gpsmm’

Compilation exited abnormally with code 1 at Sun Sep  4 08:52:00
--------------------------------------------------

or try:

man libgpsmm

Depending on the version of gpsd, the libgpsmm man page may be out of
date. libgps no longer requires a callback, but the libgpsmm man page
calls it out. Of course the source is authoritative.

Or, if you aren't wedded to C++, take a look at gpxlogger.c.
