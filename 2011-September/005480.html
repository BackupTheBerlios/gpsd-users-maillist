Steve you are using three terms bearing, track an heading. Let me point 
out the meaning.

track is a set of point where you've been in the past.
Garmin also uses track as an synonym for Corse over Ground
Bearing is the angle from your position to the selected waypoint.

Heading is the angle from the ship heading to the north pole.

Let me say it simple. You can drive down the road from A to B having the 
same track, the same course over ground driving one foreward and the 
second time driving from A to B using the reverse gear. You will have 
two very different headings (and reactions from the bystander).

A GPS will not see the difference between both tests.

There are GPSs [1] how can "see" a heading. They have two (or more) 
aerial. This allows to compare two very alike but not equal positions. 
This difference gives the heading.

Does that help?

[1] <http://www.kongsberg-simrad.de/pdf/kompass_hs50_eng.pdf>
