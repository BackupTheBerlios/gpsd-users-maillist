Dear GPSD-users group:

I have a marine GPS receiver that uses a sentence identifier that GPSD 
doesn't seem to recognize. The contents of an example NMEA sentence is 
identical to $GPGGA, but the identifier is $ECGGA. It's my understanding 
that the first two characters identify the talker type (and "GP" refers 
to a GPS receiver) and the last three characters identify the sentence 
contents.

I don't happen to know what "EC" refers to, but the GPS receiver is an 
otherwise normal NMEA GPS talker. Here are example NMEA sentences from 
this receiver:

$ECGGA,210650,4801.834,N,12244.747,W,2,07,1.0,0.0,M,-17.0,M,1.0,0000,D*2F
$ECGLL,4801.834,N,12244.747,W,210650,A,D*46
$ECRMC,210650,A,4801.834,N,12244.747,W,0.0,011.5,070911,018.5,E,D*1D
$ECVTG,011.5,T,353.0,M,0.0,N,0.0,K,D*37

It seems that the only difference between these sentences and 
conventional NMEA-0183 sentences is the "EC" prefix. And as it happens, 
most marine GPS navigation equipment that must read NMEA sentences has 
the ability to read the sentences as formatted by this receiver.

So here's my request -- would it be possible to code GPSD to accept any 
"talker" specifier, as in the above example, rather than limiting itself 
to the "GP" specifier?

Thanks for reading!
