> Gary E. Miller wrote:

>> Can you provide a sample of the bad GPGSA sentences?  This sort of thing

> Here's a sample, from the gpsd/Zaurus thread:
>
>   gpsd: <= GPS: $GPGSA,A,1,,,,*32
>
> As you can see, several (blank) fields are missing; they correspond to 
> the 12 satellite data fields.
>
> Actually, Eric has already patched nmea_parser.c to handle it; his fix 
> is to simply ignore sentences that are not of the expected length.
>
I had a problem with this fix and had to remove it from my personal 
version of gpsd. I have 4 different gps and 3 of them produces messages 
of the same type with different numbers of fields.

I guess no one has or is willing to pay for a copy of the official NMEA 
standard but my guess is that on some sentences the order of the fields 
are statically defined but in some cases the number of a certain field 
is variable or a sentence can be cut short.

I was fixing up the code last night to handle the RMC sentence. In this 
sentence I see one of my units does not supply the last three fields 
which specify the magnetic variation, direction and FAA indicator. With 
the GSA sentence one unit can output up to 14 satellites followed by 
PDOP,HDOP,VDOP where my other units have spaces for 12 and may or maynot 
supply the PDOP,HDOP,VDOP.

This NMEA "standard" is not very standard at all.
