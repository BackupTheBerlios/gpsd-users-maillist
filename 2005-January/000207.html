On Thu, 2005-01-13 at 18:46 -0500, Eric S. Raymond wrote:
> > Are you suggesting to support the whole set of specifiers from
> > strftime()? The remaining %f, %L,  %N, %o, %q, %Q, %v are certainly not
> > enough to format the GPS data.
> 
> I see.  That complicates matters.
>  
> > What I could do is to have '%' for anything time related, and some other
> > character ('$'?) as format specifier for GPS related stuff.
> 
> Ick.  I guess we'll have to do that.

An alternative would be to only support what ANSI C strftime() has to
offer. That's 21 specifiers, leaving 31 for other stuff. The downside is
that we can not just pass everything beginning with an '%' on to
strftime and use whatever the platform has to offer. The upside is only
one character used, and ANSI probably covers most, if not all, cases of
what other programs expect as input.
