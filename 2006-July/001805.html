Hi David,

What you are asking for is a GIS function, that is included in many
GIS/mapping packages out there.
What one does is snap the gps points to a vector network that
represents the roads.

I think it doesn't make any sense to incluse this on gpsd, since that
deviation should be time and position dependent, and also map
dependent .
Most of the time, we have maps that were made with a scale, and are
only accurate to that scale. GPS also have an accuracy, but as the
position is indicated up to the centimetre range, we rely on that when
we should trust it up to 50m.

So, your position error is well within the expected accuracy of the
Global Positioning System.
