> But there's one problem I encounter: As soon as a process is connected
> to the serial port, it cannot disconnect anymore. "gpsd" hangs on
> disconnecting sometimes also "cat /dev/ttyr00". I even can't kill -9 the
> process gpsd, reload the module or continue using that port.
> The only way to restart gpsd is to restart the computer.


The primary reason that kill -9 won't work is because the driver is
stuck in a non-interruptible wait of some type, typically due to a
driver bug.

If you have SysRq support built in, you can try SysRq-T to figure out
what address the process is blocked on and report back to the Moxa
developers. You may need to do Ctrl-Alt-SysRq on a PC, or Ctrl-Break
on a serial console to do the SysRq.

SysRq-D says that it shows all locks, which might also have some useful info.
