> gpsd *gets* altitude and then throws it away.  That's the problem, as
> I recall.  Pretty sure that (altitude) shows in the raw NMEA dump I

gpsd *never* 'throws away' altitude present in the NMEA.  You can
prove this to yourself by looking at the nmea_parse.c code.

Any time altitude is in the NMEA, gpsd will forward it to the client
until the fix timestamp changes and altitude becomes invalid.
Altitude is forwarded even if mode is not 3D, because GPSes sometime
have enough ddata to interpolate altitude for a vcfew minutes after
they lose 3D lock.
