David Findlay <david at davsoft.com.au>:
>I'm having various problems with GPSd. I have a Haicom HI-305N gps which plugs
>into my laptop via the serial port. Windows apps have no trouble using it in 
>NMEA mode. Running GPSd with no additional configuration other than pointing 
>it at /dev/ttyS0 causes some problems. Running xgps the lock is lost 
>immediately after every update until the next one comes in.  So the lat and 
>lon etc all disappear after less than a second until the next update comes. 

This is a known problem; gpsd by default does not assume that data held over
from the last cycle is good.  cgps has a 'j' command to change this behavior,
but xgps does not yet.

> Other programs such as kismet constantly say no fix, sometimes for
> 10s of seconds until they suddenly get a fix which again disappears
> in a fraction of a second. From using this device in Windows it
> seems to update once a second.

This part I don't understand.

> Anyone know what my problem may be? Thanks,

HI-305's output I may be able to figure it out.
