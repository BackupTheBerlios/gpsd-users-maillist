In message <Pine.LNX.4.64.0609271144430.26287 at catbert.rellim.com> you write:

>> the Garmin 18 LVC really outputs +/- 5v? you mean they finally got it
>> right?!

>Looking at the GPS-18/LVC spec.

>Section 1.5.3: The unit accepts TIA-232-F (RS-232) level inputs and
>transmits voltage levels that swing from ground to the positive supply
>coltage, TIA-232-F (RS-232) polarity.

So, no, the data output is ground (zero) to plus five volts 
(nominal +5V supply), not minus five to plus five volts.



>Section 1.6.2.1: Input Voltage
>  GPS-18/LVC: 4.0 - 5.5V

>Section 1.6.2.2 Input Current
>  GPS-18/LVC: 60mA @ 5.0V

Uh, these are  the voltage/current that power the unit, and are
not related to the *output* drive level.



>Section 1.6.2.3 CMOS Serial Output Levels
>  GPS-18/LVC: 0V to Vin, between 4 and 5.5V (Asynchronous Serial, TIA-232-F
>              (RS-232) Compatible Polarity)

I use a "max232" style buffer/driver to the PC, on a Trimble SV6 which has
similar cmos buffer output driver spec, as this gps18lvc. 

More about PC hardware and OEM GPS interfacing is at:

http://www.wraith.sf.ca.us/ntp/
