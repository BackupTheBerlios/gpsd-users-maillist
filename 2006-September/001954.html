> > The RMC, GLL, and GGA sentences all include position information. Parsing

> > only GGA on this unit.

> Not really.  If you check the output samples we have collected in gpsd

> the same time stamp.  So they are just slightly different ways of saying

As you can see from the above, it sends them separately, at different times.  
I initially assumed that it would send one sentence per second, but it 
appears to be sending them in subgroups, one subgroup per second. I haven't 
determined the exact subgrouping yet. The need for better timestamping is 
obvious.


> gpsd has stayed away from interpolation of data.  The intent has been to
> just provide the facts to the client and let the client do any fancy
> processing of the data.
>

I'm sorry if I was unclear. I didn't mean that I thought gpsd should handle 
these processing tasks. I was just outlining the data processing steps that 
will be required for the application. These steps certainly should be handled 
by the client.

My system outputs only DPT, but NMEA has at least four different depth 
sentences specified, just to make life more interesting:
DBK - Depth Below Keel
DBT - Depth Below Transducer
DBS - Depth Below Surface
DPT - Depth
And http://www.nmea.de/nmea0183datensaetze.html has DPT mislabeled 
as "Heading", although the format shown is correct.
