> see.
> 
> The apps I came across all call gpsd (e.g GpsDrive or qpeGPS) with -s 4800.
> On Linux that switch is simply not recognised (invalid option -- s). 
> On FreeBSD gpsd starts, but doesn't deliver any data.
> 
> And while -D2 and -N does what it should on a Linux system, it doesn't on

-s is an obsolete switch that fixes the baud rate to the device.  
2.32 and other moderbn versions autobaud,

By what you say, the FreeBSD port is pretty far out of date, probably from
the 1.x series.  That should be fixed first.
