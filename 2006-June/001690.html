Joerg Ostertag <Gpsd-users at ostertag.name>:
> using gpxlogger for http://www.openstreetmp.org/ and trying to import it
> into josm i recognized that the time string has a strange format:
> 
> ------------------------------------------------------
>   <trkseg>
>    <trkpt lat="47.950730" lon="11.573513">
>     <ele>678.400000</ele>
>     <time>2006-06-04T14:02:42Z</time>
>     <fix>3d</fix>
>    </trkpt>
> ------------------------------------------------------
>  - Is the Z at the end of the time string on purpose?
>  - Or was it intended to be the TimeZone?

It's on purpose.  It designates Zulu or GMT time.  What you're
looking at is ISO 8601 time format, the international standard.

Look in gpsutils.c for C code to translate this into Unix time.
