Yo Wangzhe!

I am looking at your data and do not see any problem.  What is your
problem?

On Wed, 28 Jun 2006, Wangzhe wrote:

> I had the same "lost PPS lock" problem as Tim did before. But I got another
> trouble after running ntpdate, ntpq gives:
>
> ntpq> peer
>      remote           refid      st t when poll reach   delay   offset
> jitter
> ==============================================================================
> xSHM(0)          .GPS.            0 l    6   16  377    0.000  695.148
> 252.455
> xSHM(1)          .GPS1.           0 l    9   16  177    0.000  -15.181
> 11.485

That looks good.  The offset is not right, but my guess is you have not
adjusted your fudge yet.  Just add 300ms or subtract 700ms at try
again.

What about your other ntp servers?

> ntpq> asso
>
> ind assID status  conf reach auth condition  last_event cnt
> ===========================================================
>   1 63884  9124   yes   yes  none falsetick   reachable  2
>   2 63885  9124   yes   yes  none falsetick   reachable  2

That should go away once you get the fudge set relative to your other
ntpd servers.  What are your other servers showing?

> The output of gpsd with -D 5:
>
> gpsd: Satellite data OK.
> gpsd: carrier-detect on /dev/ttyS1 changed to 1
> gpsd: ntpshm_pps: precision -5
> gpsd: polling 4
> gpsd: carrier-detect on /dev/ttyS1 changed to 0

Looks good.

> Does anyone have the idea of the "falsetick"?  The NTP doc explains: 'The
> peer is discarded by the intersection algorithm as a falseticker', which
> does not help me much.

It just means that the time from those two does not agree with your
other ntp servers.  Get the fudge set in and that will go away.
