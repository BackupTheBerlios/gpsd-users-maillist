On Tue, Sep 9, 2008 at 1:20 PM, David Foo <davidxfoo at gmail.com> wrote:
> Dear,
>
>   I have some weird project on GPS. I need to know (i) when a satellite
> sends
>   a signal, (ii) when the GPS receives the signal from the satellite, (iii)
>   the coordinate of the satellite.
>   Could I get this information using gpsd?

Maybe. This sounds a little like you want to do a software solver and
what you're asking for is raw measurements. Unfortunately that's
hardware-dependent: some receivers will give you that, some won't.
Internally gpsd won't do anything with those measurements, but if you
put gpsd into super-raw mode, you can pass through the vendor binary
protocol which may have the quantities you're looking for. Of course,
you'd have to parse the data yourself - gpsd doesn't have a nice
raw-measurement format. It's something I've been thinking about, but
slacking on.
