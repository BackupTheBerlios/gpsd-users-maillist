On Sun, 14 Sep 2008, Adam Dosch wrote:

>      remote           local      st poll reach  delay   offset    disp
> =======================================================================
> =SHM(0)          127.0.0.1        0   64  377 0.00000 -0.339167 0.03044
> =c-76-115-181-18 192.168.0.16     1   64  377 0.08670 -0.061885 0.05359
> *ntp-nasa.arc.na 192.168.0.16     1   64  337 0.06383 -0.056781 0.05219

That does not look right.  You should have 2 SHM().  Like this:

# ntpdc -p
     remote           local      st poll reach  delay   offset    disp
=======================================================================
=SHM(0)          127.0.0.1        0   16  377 0.00000  0.005976 0.01590
*SHM(1)          127.0.0.1        0   16  377 0.00000  0.000000 0.01479
=bigben.cac.wash 204.17.205.18    1   64  377 0.00987  0.001055 0.06242
=clepsydra.dec.c 204.17.205.18    1   64  377 0.02864  0.000696 0.04440
=clock.isc.org   204.17.205.18    1   64  377 0.03003 -0.007132 0.05742

The first one, SHM(0), is NMEA time, from the serial stream.  The
second one is the PPS, SHM(1).

Recheck your ntp.conf.  Then add about 300mS fudge to your NMEA time.
