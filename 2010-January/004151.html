We don't know how to do this.

It requires picking up and interpreting information broadcast by
cellphone towers, so it would at minimum need another receiver
(separate from the GPS mouse) operating in those frequency bands. Of
course, a cellphone has one of those built in.

There's another level of the problem, too. Supposing we had the right sort
of receiver, AGPS systems are carrier-dependent.  As far as we know 
there isn't any standard for the format of the corrections.

I think you're the third person to bring this up.   I should probably
do a FAQ entry on this.
