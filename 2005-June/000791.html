> The longer it takes to do the reflash, the more chance that the
> cat/child/S.O./ConEd has to disrupt the process and leave you with a
> fancy paper-weight. Having said that, if "slow but easy" means less bug
> prone, then perhaps it is better than "fast but complicated".

Plenty of other things (your dsl router, bios, raid card, etc.) can
take quite a while to flash. The industry consensus seems to be "don't
reflash unless this fixes an unbearable problem, as the flash could
break things even worse. if something breaks, we warned you sucka!"
And for the most part, the world is happy with that. Not that this is
a good thing, that's just how it looks to me.

I guess the feeling I'm getting is "faster is better, but safety and
reliability are paramount." Which means no dawdling loading up code,
but no hackish shortcuts to unnecessarily save a millisecond. Gotcha.
