"Eric S. Raymond" <esr at thyrsus.com> writes:
> Do you have an interface specification for Car-CAN-Bus?

Frame formats for canbus can be found from a few sites.  The problem
appears to be that the actual data-values for the commands transported
over canbus aren't well defined.  They seem to be up to the individual
user.

The good news is that canbus packets tend to be simple and something
like the speedometer msg will be easy to reverse engineer.  It will be
the only message that gets emitted once or twice a second, is more or
less a constant when on cruise control and has one number in the
packet that varies linearly with speed.

Here are a few interesting links:

    http://www.can-cia.org/canopen/
    http://www.canopen.us/
    http://www.esacademy.com/faq/docs/canopen/

    http://canopen.sourceforge.net/  (a bit stale at this point)

There are a few sources for canbus PCMCIA cards, but they all seem to
be priced for the engineering prototype market at prices like $1500
per card.  I couldn't bring myself to buy a $3 canbus chip on a card,
for these prices.

I'm pretty sure I read that some cars have the cell phones and
on-board GPS connected to the canbus.  If enough of the protocol could
be sprung free from the responsible companies it would open up a whole
world of possibilities for gpsd.  It might even be possible to relay
the built-in gps's information via the built-in cell phone and track
the car from the net.
