Not to depress anyone from doing this, but CAN in a vehicle is very specific
to each variant and even model year. Further, there are at least two
variants of the physical layer that I know of, with different speeds
(normally 100kbaud). And this stuff is normally confidential, so it must be
reverse engineered.

On the other hand, would it be more useful that GPSD provides an interface
for providing extra information (be it speed, bearing, etc.) in addition? I
guess wheel speed sensors could also provide some extra precision and it
doesn't put vehicle specific implementations into GPSD, but into an
application that might want to improve the performance of GPSD somehow.

Are there any CAN controllers for Linux? I've looked at some PC104, but
there doesn't seem too much (admittedly, I haven't looked too deeply
either).

Cheers,
Jason.
