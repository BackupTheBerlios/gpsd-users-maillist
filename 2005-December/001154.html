> History of Navigation:
> 
> Back in the day, there was this thing called inertial navigation. It 
> worked well enough to send men to the moon and back, put large
> missiles  to within a few feet of their target from halfway around the
> world, and  other various and sundry tasks. 

and before that there was "dead reckoning" navigation. Travel a certain
distance in a certain direction for a certain amount of time, and you
get to point "B". Add in your best guess at wind or ocean current
corrections for slight improvements. It worked well enough to get
Magellan around the world in a leaky wooden boat with no electronics,
and James Cook around three times with just a wind-up clock.
