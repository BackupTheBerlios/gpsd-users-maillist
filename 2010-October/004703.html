Hello all,

I am a new subscriber of this list and a user of gpsd since 
September, 2010. I am using gpsd to provide a Pulse Per Second to 
ntpd over the shared memory interface.

I am also using a Trimble Placer 450 GPS receiver that is configured 
to generate NMEA sentances. I understand that the maintainers of gpsd 
requested notification regarding the use of new GPS receivers and if 
this is not the correct forum to do so, please let me know.

I have encountered a problem with gpsd interpretation of the NMEA 
sentences generated by the Placer 450. The Placer 450 reports time of 
day in the GLL, GGA, ZDA and RMC sentances. However, only the ZDA 
sentence reports the current time of day. In the other 3 sentences, 
the time of day is the time the information in the sentence was 
valid. If the antenna is disconnected, the time in the GLL, GGA and 
RMC sentences stops advancing as the data reported becomes stale. The 
time in the ZDA sentence continues to advance.

This creates a problem for ntpd as gpsd reports the time in all 4 
sentences to ntpd as the current time of day. Ntpd sees this as time 
running backwards and becomes very confused. This problem was 
corrected by modifying the source code (driver_nmea.c) to report only 
the time in the ZDA sentence to ntpd.

I also noticed a daily cycle in the time offset data (loopstats) from 
ntpd, almost 200 us peak-peak. Does anyone know if this performance 
is typical? The status of my time server, including plots of 
loopstats data, can be viewed at http://69.204.213.233:22675/ntpd/

Thanks,
Bruce
