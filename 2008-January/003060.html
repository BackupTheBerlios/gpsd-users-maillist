Dave C <gpsd at creativesilicon.com.au>:
> Unit 2 is currently sitting on my desk, waiting to be shipped (tomorrow, of 
> course). It too was working perfectly up until end of year. However, this 
> unit is now absolutely adamant that the date is 2008-01-02 - i.e. one day 
> LATER than it really is. I've power cycled it, and upgraded the firmware as 
> a last resort, but GPSD still tells me that the date is 2 Jan, not 1 Jan.

Rob Janssen reported this this morning.  Seems our implementation of mkgmtime()
was defective, but we never noticed before because it was introduced after 
the last leap-day.

I have committed a fix to SVN.  I'm going to cut a release today.  If you
can, please test immediately to check that the fix works in your environment.
