Hi,

while we're on the topic, a general plea, which will become more
important as more folks use survey grade RTK & DGPS signals-

output lat/lon as %.7f (or more) not %f, so we don't lose precision in
the output stage.


%f: 1852 m/nm * 60nm/degree_lat * 1e-6 = 11cm. Survey grade GPSs can
measure to ~2mm in the horizontal, and an order of magnitude worse than
that in the vertical.

so %.7f will give you 1cm, %.8f to 1mm, and %.9f to 0.1mm, which you'll
want if the position is good to ~1mm and you don't want to worry about
bluring it with rounding errors.


I was noticing this yesterday with cgpxlogger. (hey, cgpxlogger is
great; but the man page in 2.34 is missing what the -j CaSoC command
line option does)
