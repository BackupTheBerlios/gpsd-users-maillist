Generally, you should assume GPS will not work indoors. There are some
modern, high-sensitivity receivers that do well: I have a sirf3 that
occasionally gets a fix inside parkades, and I have an Antaris4 that
gets a fix inside my office building. But that takes a while and in
both of these poor reception environments I have very high error
estimates.

So part of my standard GPS troubleshooting procedure is to go outside
and try it someplace with an unobstructed view of the sky, or at least
with nothing higher than 45 degrees elevation.

Also, I'd like to suggest that you try gpsd 2.35beta from SVN. This
has the -b flag which you may find useful because it will prevent gpsd
from trying to reconfigure your GPS. Some (many? all?) bluetooth
receivers don't like having their communications protocol reset; often
they are left unable to communicate until you pull the batteries -
including the 2 week backup battery soldered onboard. Just to let you
know...
