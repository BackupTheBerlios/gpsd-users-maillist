If your Geode board has an IDE interface brought out, try using a Compact 
Flash card as the boot device. A CF card (with the appropriate adapter) 
will plug straight into the IDE bus and appears in Linux as /dev/hd<x>. 
There's not hot-swappable when used in an IDE adapter though - the kernel 
gets *very* confused!
Sandisk make a range of high speed CF cards called 'Ultra II'. Off the top 
of my head, they have read/write speeds approaching 10 MBit/sec. One of 
these may provide you with a faster boot time. They are also cheaper to 
replace than onboard flash if you wear them out during development! Sandisk 
also rate them down to -20C - and I've tested them in equipment in 
Antarctica.....
