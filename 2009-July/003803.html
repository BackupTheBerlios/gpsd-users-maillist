Hiya,

>I see ground, tx, rx, but no +5V ??

RS232 DB9 connectors do not provide any power (i.e. 5V) at all. You 
will have to use the appropriate (for your GPS) external power 
supply. Check the datasheet for what the GPS requires.

The other issue is that it's not clear if the GPS outputs true RS232 
level signals (i.e. -30 to -3V, +3 to +30V), or if it just outputs 
TTL RS232 level signals (0 & (3v3 or 5V). If it's the former, you'll 
be able to use your USB-DB9 adapter cable via a simple PS2->DB9 
adapter. If it's the latter, you're going to need to use a level 
translator chip such as a MAX232 (5V) or MAX3232 (3V3) between the 
GPS and the DB9 connector.

You might just be better off trying to get the correct cable for the 
GPS from ebay or a dealer....

BTW, I don't own one of these, nor do I have access to one so I can't 
help much more than this.

Hope this helps.
