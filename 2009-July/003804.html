Dave wrote:
> The other issue is that it's not clear if the GPS outputs
> true RS232 level signals (i.e. -30 to -3V, +3 to +30V),
> or if it just outputs TTL RS232 level signals (0 & (3v3 or
> 5V). If it's the former, you'll be able to use your USB-DB9
> adapter cable via a simple PS2->DB9 adapter. If it's the
> latter, you're going to need to use a level translator chip
> such as a MAX232 (5V) or MAX3232 (3V3) between the GPS and the
> DB9 connector.

if you need this or not depends on the computer you plug it
into. In my experience many desktop PCs with a real serial UART
will handle TTL signals (0|5v; inverse) but often laptops and
PCMCIA serial expander cards are more strict and only allow
true RS-232.
