But, what I'm surprised about is that gpsd didn't fail on the first
sentence with 13 sats in it.  It waited until the 4th line.  Looks like
this GPS receiver is listing sats that it's "not tracking" as well.  I
guess now that we have the WAAS signal we occasionally may have more
than 12 satellites in view.  So, it looks like the NMEA standard is yet
again aging.  We may want to support this sats>12 extension.

# GPS-35:
# $GPGSV,<1>,<2>,<3>,<4>,<5>,<6>,<7>,...<4>,<5>,<6>,<7>*hh
# 1) Total number of GSV sentences to be transmitted
# 2) Number of current GSV sentence
# 3) Total number of satellites in view, 00 to 12 (leading zeros sent)
# 4) Satellite PRN number, 01 to 32 (leading zeros sent)
# 5) Satellite elevation, 00 to 90 degrees (leading zeros sent)
# 6) Satellite azimuth, 000 to 359 degrees, true (leading zeros sent)
# 7) Signal to Noise ratio (C/No) 00 to 99 dB, null when not tracking
