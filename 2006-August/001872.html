Multipath, interference and bad antenna connections come quickly to
mind. While your receiver might have a good optical view of the sky,
it could be that the radio reception is poor.

> Here is some output I get from gpspipe:

> GPSD,Y=GSV ? 4:6 32 275 0 0:2 31 130 0 0:26 78 306 0 0:1 62 210 0 0:

4SVs visible
PRN 6 at 32/275, SNR 0, not used in solution

> $GPGSV,1,1,04,06,32,275,00,02,31,130,00,26,78,306,00,01,62,210,00*70
1/1 satellite view lines, pretty much exactly what the Y report says

> $GPGSA,A,1,,,,,,,,,,,,,,,*1E

In short, your receiver is connected, but it doesn't have a fix.
