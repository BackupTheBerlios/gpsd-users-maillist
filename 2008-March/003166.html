Matt Roberds <mattroberds at cox.net>:
> On Sun, 9 Mar 2008, Lixus Zoran wrote:
> > I am using gpsd 2.30-1ubuntu3 together with viking for real time
> > tracking.  I have a Garmin eTrex connected to an USB2Serial dongle
> > and I am using NMEA.  That all works fine but if I close the lid
> > of my Laptop it is suspending to RAM to save battery power. If I
> > wake it up again, gpsd hangs and therefore also viking hangs:
> 
> I wonder if the laptop is turning off the USB devices to save power,
> and the USB-to-serial device (or even the eTrex) is getting confused.

This is almost certainly the case.  USB drivers are a notoriously 
bad trouble spot in Linux suspend/resume, to the point where one of the
standard hacks if you can't get it to work is to custonize your 
suspend script to rmmod USB support from the kernel.  

Alas, there is nothing gpsd can do about this.  The kernel will
have to improve to fix it.

Chris, you might consider adding this to the Upstream Bugs page.
