On Mon, 10 Mar 2008, Lixus Zoran wrote:
> I guess I have to look for a kernel patch for pl2303.c
> or a new usb 2 serial adaptor whose kernel driver supports suspend.

Some other options:

- Use a serial port on a PCMCIA/PC Card or Express Card.  These are more
   expensive than a USB-to-serial adapter, but may behave better through
   suspend/resume.  The kernel may still try to shut off the card slots,
   though.

- Power the USB-to-serial adapter from a +5 V source that doesn't go
   away when the laptop is suspended.  If your laptop still has a PS/2
   mouse/keyboard connector, there may be +5 V on that.  This will
   require building a custom cable.  You could even power the adapter
   from its own battery.
