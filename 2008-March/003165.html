On Sun, 9 Mar 2008, Lixus Zoran wrote:
> I am using gpsd 2.30-1ubuntu3 together with viking for real time
> tracking.  I have a Garmin eTrex connected to an USB2Serial dongle
> and I am using NMEA.  That all works fine but if I close the lid
> of my Laptop it is suspending to RAM to save battery power. If I
> wake it up again, gpsd hangs and therefore also viking hangs:

I wonder if the laptop is turning off the USB devices to save power,
and the USB-to-serial device (or even the eTrex) is getting confused.
To check: Don't run gpsd at all, but instead launch minicom or a
similar terminal emulator.  Point minicom at the serial port of your
GPS device, adjust bit rate, parity, etc, and you should see the NMEA
sentences coming in.  Close the lid, wait for the laptop to suspend,
and then open the lid again.  If the NMEA sentences resume, possibly
after a few seconds delay, then the problem may indeed be in gpsd.
If the NMEA sentences never resume, then maybe your USB-to-serial
dongle or kernel drivers are getting confused; you might look at the
output of dmesg for clues.

> I would love to test against newest gpsd but unfortunately gpsd from
> svn does not compile on my ubuntu dapper 6.06.

You should be able to get a little newer than 2.30; have you tried
downloading the tarball of 2.37 and compiling that?
