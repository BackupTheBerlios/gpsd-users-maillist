I believe so.

With no gps receiver present, I killed off a running gpsd, and
moved /etc/default/gpsd aside. I then check for /dev/ttyUSB* present;
there were none. I fired up my client, which will re-try gpsd if it
can't find it.

I then plugged in my receiver, a BU-353. gpsd launched, and my client
picked it up immediately, and found the gps receiver.

This was with GPSD version: 2.96~dev Rev: 2010-11-21T22:19:16, Protocol
3.3, running on a fresh install of Ubuntu 10.10, Maverick.

I notice that the new command line is

gpsd -F /var/run/gpsd.sock

Sometimes I like to have -G in there as well. I'll look at a graceful
way to get that in there.
