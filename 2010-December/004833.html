I have found a USB GPPS to test on.  The internal RS-232 is indeed just
4800 bps.  This uses a pl2303 like the other reported problem cases and
the pl2303 linux driver actually reports that the link is running at
4800 bps.  In the past I had seen the 4800 in stty and thought it was
just a dummy value.

I have also seen gpsd autobauding on USB GPS and thought it was a
pointless waste of time, but now I see it really is required.  If I
change the pl2303 speed the GPS data I see is bad and gpsd will
autobaud until the data is good.

So the original test for minimum bps is all that needs to be done.  I
thought I had seen USB-RS-232 converters that were not reporting
correct bps to linux and will keep an eye out for that case.

Now I am wondering if I could program the SiRF to a higher speed, set
the pl2303 to match, and get good results that way.
