> Do you have good skyview? Any tall buildings either obstructing parts
> of the sky or possibly causing reflections?

It's a residential (single-family home) neighborhood, there are trees 
(currently leafless, though), no aluminum/steel siding. OK view of the sky 
above, and to the southwest. At some point (when I have a laptop 
available), I'll take it somewhere where it'll have a full sky view, and 
see if that settles it down (but it's not sounding good).

I rubber-banded the receiver to a board and stuck it out the window. It 
seems to have limited the vertical drift to a 30M range, and the 
horizontal drift has settled down to the last two digits of the lat + lon 
seconds.

There's still a (fairly constant) 2 block offset west-northwest. 
This is based on using Google Maps, though, and I have no idea what 
projection they're using. Offsets are fine for my application, as long
as they're consistant.
