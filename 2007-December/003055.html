Chris Kuethe wrote:
> Wanna bet someone is interpreting the location format wrong? ie.
> DDD.MMSSss vs DDD.dddddd? That's been the cause of a few
> off-by-2-blocks errors in the past.

If the error is steady and on the order of 100-150m it is usually a
incorrect map datum to blame. If the error is jumping around in the
area of 30 miles or so, then DDD.MMSSss vs DDD.dddddd is likely.

DDD:MM.MMM vs DDD:MM:SS.SSS mistakes will give you an error of about
1/2 a mile..
