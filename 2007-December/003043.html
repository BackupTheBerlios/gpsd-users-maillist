> An idea for a new exercise: put tabs on the right side too, one of which will

   I thought about it, but if I'm not mistaken, showing a Google map
would require embedding an entire browser in the application (given that
Google maps is heavily dependent on Javascript, fairly advanced CSS,
etc., way beyond your stock HTML rendering widget).  This in my view is
a level of complexity far above of what is needed for what should in
essence be a simple test client for gpsd.

   Glad however to know that you have found something which does what
you describe, albeit not for gpsd.  Care to post a link for us?  I did a
search but I couldn't find the application you mentioned--only the
"original" Maemo Mapper.  As Derrick says, it's probably easier to add
gpsd support to their app rather than GMaps support to qtgpsc.

   Anyway, what I have done in v0.2.0 is add a "Copy as KML" link which
lets you paste your gpsd position in Google Earth.  Not a live feed,
granted, but it's a start.
