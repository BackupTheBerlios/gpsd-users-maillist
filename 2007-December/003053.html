Try that Trimble planning app - You could be in a place/time where
your reception sucks.

Random antennas, satellite dishes, power lines and other metallic
objects can also impact GPS reception.

So can temperature. My BU-303 was rated down to -20C; colder than that
and it would report weaker signals and larger errors, probably due to
the onboard clock slowing and the patch antenna shrinking. I never
noticed much of a problem with precipitation - rain, snow or ice.

Something else you can try: let your receiver drive gpsd for 24hrs,
and log the output with "gpspipe -rv > mysite.txt". It'll be big. Then
feed the log to the skyview.php script included with the gpsd
distribution. You'll get some neat graphs like this - they might
explain what you're seeing.
http://www.ualberta.ca/~ckuethe/gps/skyviews/

Wanna bet someone is interpreting the location format wrong? ie.
DDD.MMSSss vs DDD.dddddd? That's been the cause of a few
off-by-2-blocks errors in the past.

I can understand the desire to go cheap, but I keep finding new
receivers to hack on. Maybe get a UT-41; they're $35 or so.

Like Matt Roberds says, trade money for time. If you're willing to
sample a given location for a few minutes to an hour at a time, you
can get a pretty good idea of where you are, with high confidence.
