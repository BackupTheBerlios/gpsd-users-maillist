On Dec 11, 2007 4:02 AM, Eric S. Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com> wrote:
> Chris (who should correct me if I have any of this wrong):
> 1. pipe mode -- I think this is Chris's proposal for a revived -T option
> or something similar.

something like that. point gpsd at a file full of raw gps capture, and
out comes gpsd-format messages. at least two of us have our uses for
this.

> 4. new driver: ashtech -- Support for the Ashtech chips often used
> in embedded navigation systems.

and there's a patch to do this in the debian tracker. should probably
follow up w/ the submitter and mention that gpsd is bsd-licensed and
check that they're ok with that before merging the patch.

> 5. new output format for hi-rez postprocessing -- not certain what this
> is about but it might mesh with our to-to item of tweaking the output
> formats so they can play nice with millimeter-precision GPSes.

part of it is not throwing away inbound precision data. the other part
of it is contingent on the next generation protocol: i want to add raw
measurement output in some common format so that toolkits like gpstk
and gipsy can be used.

> 6. fix the Trimble support -- something is badly wrong in the TSIP/Trimble
> drivers.  Their regression tests are so unstable that I've taken them
> out of the qualification sequence for release and added a warning to the
> bug list in TODO.  This should be fixed; Chris muttered about doing it
> during an upcoming trip to Mexico.

why mexico? because i can actually sit outside with my laptop and work
on it. gps hacking in a wintery urban canyon is no fun.
