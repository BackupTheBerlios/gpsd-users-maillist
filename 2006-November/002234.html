On 11/10/06, Carl Brown <cbsled at verizon.net> wrote:
> Another possible scenario would be to keep gpsd at simple PVT, and fork the
> project with a "navd" or so branch that would be intended to handle all known
> NMEA sentences. Users with simple needs would be better served by a
> non-bloated gpsd, while the advanced stuff would be available for those who
> need it.

One more vote for It's A Bad Idea.

Having a daemon that can handle PVT in most of the forms you're likely
to find on the market is a good thing. We make it easy to strip out
drivers you don't need. To use a bit of an oversimplification: bloat
is what happens when you have no control over what gets installed.
Modular is the same package, but with fine-grained controls.

We write BSD-licensed code, do with it what you will... but I'm not
interested in forking.
