2006/11/7, Eric S. Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com>:

My ideas may not be worth a penny, since I havent contributed nothing
to the project, but this is interesting stuff anyway...

> 1. GPSD is about PVT information from GPSes only.

OK, this was the case prior to TNT...

> 2. GPSD is about facilitating multi-application access to anything that
>    comes out of a supported device.

This is a bit too broad...it may as well include other onboard
instruments, such as accelorometers, temperature and pressure
gauges...

> 3. GPSD is about PVT information and any other kind of information associated
>    closely enough with PVT that applications will want to treat them as a
>    functional unit.

I would summary this to PVT+attitude information (or you could call
this theory 4). This way, you include TNT or other compasses,
artificial horizons, but exclude other sensors (temp press in previous
example) or naviation issues that are clearly application issues.
If that includes bathymetry depends if you consider it positional (or
attitude) information, as may be the case with submersible vehicles.

Then you may have to be open to incorporate directional sonar, radar
and the like... Again, this is overly broad. However, they may all
relate as long as or provided) they spit NMEA.
A name change would be due in such case...
