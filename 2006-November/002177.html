On 11/7/06, Eric S. Raymond <esr at thyrsus.com> wrote:
> You may be right that this doesn't belong in GPSD.  That's partly
> a question of how we define GPSD's scope.  There are at least three
> possible theories:
>
> 1. GPSD is about PVT information from GPSes only.

Our actions says this is not true.

> 2. GPSD is about facilitating multi-application access to anything that
>    comes out of a supported device.

I think this is overly broad and fear that it could lead to feeping
creaturitis. Be careful about "anything" and what devices are
supported. ;)

> 3. GPSD is about PVT information and any other kind of information associated
>    closely enough with PVT that applications will want to treat them as a
>    functional unit.

This makes sense. DGPS receivers and NTRIP streams aren't navigation
solution sources, but they do provide augmentation information that
helps to make optimal use of the navigation solution. Given that we
like DGPS, I think supporting augmentation and metadata is not a bad
idea.

Eric is right when he asks for docs and sample hardware. Those are
critical to having a reliable "protocol x" code path.
