Another good launch today - the third Block IIR-M (Generation 2,
Replenishment, Modernized).

It seems the Air Force has learned a lot in the last year. They took
their sweet time (3 months) space-qualifying the first one (SVN53),
and understandably so. On-orbit verification of a new platform is no
small task. Still, I got a new gps *satellite* for Christmas!

A year later the second (SVN52) went up, no doubt with a whole bunch
of patches, bug fixes and case mods. As they've had a year to design a
test suite, this bird took less than 3 weeks to check out.

With everyone still wearing their "rocket man" hats, SVN58 was
launched after being delayed yesterday by weather. It wouldn't
surprise me if this one was activated sometime in the first week of
December, though official estimates are for "mid-December".

The Control Segment is to be congratulated for their expert management
of the Space Segment; many of the satellites are still functional at
twice their design lifetime. Still, there are compelling reasons to
upgrade: more efficient transmitters, higher-stability clocks, more
powerful and robust onboard computers, support for new civilian
signals...

2006 NOV 17: GPS IIR-16/M3 SVN58 PRN12 LAUNCH

2006 OCT 12: GPS IIR-15/M2 SVN52 PRN31 USABINIT
2006 SEP 25: GPS IIR-15/M2 SVN52 PRN31 LAUNCH

2005 DEC 16: GPS IIR-14/M1 SVN53 PRN17 USABINIT
