Hello all!

I recently tried to build gpsd-2.33 and got an error when doing 'make
check' - it failed when trying to compile gpsmm_test.cpp .  The problem
seems to be that line 31 of libgpsmm.h defines set_callback() as taking
a pointer to a function with four arguments, but both the forward
declaration and the definition of callback() in gpsmm_test.cpp only have
two arguments.

I fixed it by adding the len and level arguments to the forward
declaration and the definition of callback() in gpsmm_test.cpp .  I
didn't do anything with these new variables in callback() - just put
them in the argument list.

This was with gpsd-2.33.tar.gz on Debian 3.1 "Sarge", kernel 2.4.27,
and gcc 3.3.5.

Thanks!
