I've been moving our website in the direction of being a one-stop shop
for GPS programming documentation, including:

1) All relevant and publicly available standards material, and
2) links to protocol manuals for all current GPS devices we can find, and
3) archive copies of protocol manuals for obsolete and orphaned devices.

This is a useful thing to do in itself. It also gives our project an
authority it might not have otherwise.  When we claim to be the Web's 
best resource on GPS interfacing for programmers, we can back up that brag.

(I think we already host the most complete and detailed reference on
NMEA 0183 that's not the actual standard.)

Last night I cleaned up and committed documentation on the Axiom Sandpiper II,
Today, Mick Durkin sent me PDF docs on the Jupiter and Motorola devices.

I'm writing to encourage others to do likewise.  If you have documents
for some obscure and half-forgotten GPS sitting in a dusty corner of
your hard drive, and they're not already accessible from our
References page, ship them to me.

Because, as everybody who's ever heard the story of the Great Alice's 
Restaurant Massacree knows, one big pile of garbage is much more 
interesting than two little ones... :-)
