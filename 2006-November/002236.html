One thing I do wonder about.  The Sandpiper docs say the thing needs
regulated power.  Do Linksys wall warts actually deliver regulated power?

I wonder about this because the Sandpiper seems completely happy with 
the power it's getting -- I've seen no data dropouts or resets or any other
strange behavior tha might indicate it's getting voltage variations 
it dislikes.

So either the Sandpiper is more tolerant of voltage variations than the
spec sheets let on, or the wall wart is delivering regulated power, or
my mains power is unusually clean.

Or...hm...the wall wart is actually plugged into the UPS that runs 
my computer.  Could it be that whatever power smoothing the UPS is doing
is making regulation in the wall wart unnecessary?

E.g. should I add a warning in my Axiom notes that the wall-wart hack
might fail if it isn't downstream of an UPS?
