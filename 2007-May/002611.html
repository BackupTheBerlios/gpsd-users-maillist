>
> I've stopped marking devices as "excellent" ... it's either "good" or
> not.
>
> Good (good enough) means that there are likely to be drivers, we can
> easily parse the protocol and get useful data to you.  Less than good
> means that we have to do stupid tricks to get the receiver into a
> useful state, or the data is often erroneous, there are build quality
> problems, non-portable kernel modules may be needed, ...

> Your receiver have a prolific or ftdi controller - a silicon labs or
> USB ACM controller may be acceptable, though silicon laboratories
> don't release programming documentation, so open source drivers for
> this chipset may be buggy (works on my trimble and itrax though).
>
> You should be able to avoid needing crazy kernel modules (like those
> required for some magellan or garmin receivers)
>
> Your recevier should have a reasonably well documented chipset - SiRF,
> Trimble, ANTARIS, Garmin are pretty good. iTrax may also be acceptable
> - I've got a working useful iTalk decoder, but there are some gaps in
> their documentation.
>
> Depending on your usage, you may need a higher sensitivity receiver.
> SiRF3 does a pretty good job of locking on to a signal, at the cost of
