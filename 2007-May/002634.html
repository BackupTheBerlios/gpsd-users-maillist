Randy,

It occurs to me that the number of people who want to build gpsd for a
non-x86 architecture is probably only going to increase.  Once you do
get gpsd built for the avr system, a short summary of your notes on what
you had to do to get it running might help many other people.  Don't
worry that it might be specific to the AVR or to the board you're using -
stuff like how to make static libraries could be particularly valuable
to _anybody_ that's cross-compiling.

Something as simple and short as "I had to say ./configure --this --that
--not-that , and then edit the Makefile to change $FOO into $FOO_STATIC ,
and then make a symbolic link from /here to /over/there , and then it
worked" would help.
