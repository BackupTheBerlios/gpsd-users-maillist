On 5/22/07, Ingmar Frank <ingmar.frank at tesbv.com> wrote:
> Chris Kuethe schrieb:
> >> mknod /tmp/gps p
> >> softgps > /tmp/gps &
> >> gpsd -nN /tmp/gps
> >
> > Or, if you have /dev/stdin, you can do something like this:
> >
> > softgps | ./gpsd -n -N -D3 /dev/stdin
>
> Thanks for your quick answers, guys! Seems to be a lot easier than
> expected. I assume this will also work with binary data, not just NMEA,
> won't it?

Assuming we support the binary format, yes. The same methods work for
feeding in (at least) SiRF, UBX, iTalk and TSIP packets as well. If we
don't yet support your binary protocol, that's a little more
complicated, but doable - need to teach the sniffer how to recognize
your protocol, and add the requisite packet-cracking routines.
