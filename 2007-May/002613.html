> Fastrax makes the iSuite navigation software. This software can talk
> to several different chip. Quickly scanning their website, it looks
> like the mapping is this:
> 
> SiRF SiRFstarIII + isuite = itrax300
> Sony CXD2985 + isuite = itrax130
> uNav uN2110 + isuite = itrax03
> 
> That makes sense, really. Most of these receivers are a combination of
> an RF/Correlator front end, and a programmable baseband processor to
> actually generate the navigation solution and output it. This baseband
> processor tends to be something like an ARM7 or ARM9 - a general
> purpose processor.
> 
> Most vendors will ship a firmware with their receiver (SiRF->GSW3,
> uNav->ORION), but it's perfectly reasonable to port an alternative
> RTOS and navigation solver to some random ARM board.
