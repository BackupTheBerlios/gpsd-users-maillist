What version (if any) of the PL2303 driver for 2.4 kernels is reliable?

Google turns up conflicting results:  some say that the version in the 
2.4.18 kernel is okay,  some say it's not.  Some say it was fixed around 
2.4.30 or 2.4.31,  some say the driver wasn't fixed until 2.6.8.

I know the version in the 2.4.22 kernel I run on my main box is awful:  I 
can't even run gpsd with a pl2303-based device,  because trying to set 
the baud rate causes a kernel panic every time (note that the kernel 
came with some "real-time performance" patches applied that may or may 
not have touched the pl2303 driver,  so this might not be universal).  
The 2.4.26 kernel on the on the "car PC" I'm building seemed okay at 
first,  but I've had a couple of the kernel panics others have reported 
on port closing (but only a couple,  out of many dozens of tests).

Has anyone used a particular 2.4.x kernel for a long time with solid 
reliability of a pl2303-based GPS?

Thanks,

Ran
