Yeah it works pretty good.  I put the fifo somewhere where it will persist
after a reboot and respawn the gpspipe command from init.  The only
potential problem that I see is that gpspipe keeps the tcp connection to the
remote gpsd ESTABLISHED at all times.  This prevents gpsd from ever dropping
DTR on the serial port and letting the GPS receiver power down.

I may still replace gpspipe with an iptables redirect of 2947 to the remote
gpsd host to allow the power saving features to operate properly.
