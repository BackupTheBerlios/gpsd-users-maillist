>> I have three usb gps receivers based on Sirf Star III:

>> The two adapters above are a lot faster. Normally within one minute they
>> will have a fix (few seconds for a hot plug) - on worse or cloudy days, and
>> if I have obstructions arounds me - maybe 2-3 minutes. The funny thing is
>> that, the BlueNext adapter is about half of the price of most SirfStar III
>> based adapters I have seen.
>>
>> My question is: is SirfStar III a lower quality chip?
>
> No. Other devices based on SirfStar III don't have the problems you report.

I have three different devices from different manufacturers with the 
SirfStar III chipset - and all of them present these symptoms. I've just 
had a Globalsat ND100S plugged in for 32 minutes. Gpsmon shows it can 
see 4 satellites - but it never got to the point of obtaining a fix or 
seeing more satellites. I have then plugged the BueNext BN-903S in - and 
within 2 minutes it obtained a fix and could see 7 satellites. Neither 
of the two was used within the last 24 hours. All the experiment taking 
place in the same spot in my house - no movement or change of 
conditions. Within 40 minutes - thus the weather, atmospheric and 
electro-magnetic propagation conditions being roughly the same.

Saying that SirfStar III doesn't have the problems or that it is a high 
quality chip is one thing. What I see in practice, however, looks very 
different. Unless anybody can explain the significant gap in performance 
any other way.

I must be the only one noticing these differences or willing to notice them.
