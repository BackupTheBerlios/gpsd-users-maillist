Sound like what what you want is something like GPS TrackMaker for
Windows. Gpsd (& gpsdrive) are for realtime navigation in a car, plane,
boat, 12v sled, etc.

Serial port support in Crossover/Wine has traditionally been very bad.

Your GPS might be able to log track internally (eg Garmins do).

Then at the end of the day when you get home you can download any
tracks, waypoints, and routes you made along the way. You can also send
them from your computer into the GPS if you want to plan before you
leave.

In linux:

gpsman
gpsbabel
gpstrans
qgis gps plugin  qgis.org
GRASS GIS (v.in.garmin and v.in.gpsbabel modules, download only)


gpsman is probably the most featureful, qgis the easiest, and gpstrans
the simplest.
