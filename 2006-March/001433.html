On Wed, 15 Mar 2006, jmg123 wrote:

> Heres the problem

> Jeffrey$ gpsd  -D 5 -nN /dev/tty.SLAB_USBtoUART
> gpsd: launching (Version 2.30)
> gpsd: Can't bind to port gpsd
        ^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^^

That means that you likely already have a copy of gpsd running.  I
do not know of anything that grabs the gpsd port, except gpsd.

Kill your existing gpsd:
	killall gpsd

Then try to restart it again.  If that does not work then try to run
gpsd as root.
