--- Ravi Mathur <ravidavi at mail.utexas.edu> wrote:

>  Every 
> command that I send to gpsd seems to work ok, *except* for getting  
> the altitude.  If I send the 'a' command over and over, most of the  
> time it just returns "GPSD,A=?".  About 10% of the time, it will  
> actually report the correct altitude.  It seems to me that the  
> altitude is only being reported when it changes?  Is this correct?

Actually the way gpsd works is that the altitude is only reported when
the gps device reports it.  You can run gpspipe -wr and see the raw
data.  Altitude is the sixth field in the "O" (man gpsd) field and the
9th value in the GGA sentences (see NMEA).  If you want an altitude
caching capability, you'll have to put that in your application.

The gpsd list archive is at
http://lists.berlios.de/pipermail/gpsd-users.mbox/gpsd-users.mbox
download and grep it up.
