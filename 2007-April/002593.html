On Sun, 29 Apr 2007, Mark Easton wrote:
> So, you will see that my TackTick wireless interface outputs to RS232, I
> use an ATEN RS2320-USB cable to convert it to USB (my laptop does not
> have a serial interface).

Have you tried using a terminal program like Minicom to look at what the
TackTick is sending?  Don't even start gpsd - just fire up Minicom and
point it at the port - in your case, /dev/ttyUSB0 .  This can show you
if the TackTick is trying to send anything at all (and perhaps
corrupting the NMEA sentences in some way) or if it is just refusing to
send anything.

Looking at the PDF documents at
http://www.tacktick.com/2007Tacktick/products/product_view.asp?prod=46 ,
the TackTick interface claims to "pass through" the sentences GLL, RMB,
and RMC from the GPS.  Of these three, gpsd is listening for GLL and
RMC.  gpsd wants the messages to be prefixed with either GP, II, or IN
(e.g. GPGLL, IIGLL, INGLL) - if TackTick has gotten creative and is
using some other prefix, then gpsd won't "see" the sentences.  If
TackTick is using some odd prefix, you should easily see this with
Minicom or another terminal program.
