simple.  GPSD, as awesome as it is, sounds like it's overkill for *your*
application.  What you actually want is the information provided by the
NMEA sentence "GPRMC".  This single sentence issued once per second by
most GPS units contains time and location.  Have a look at this:

http://www.codepedia.com/1/The+GPRMC+Sentence

By default your unit probably spits out GPRMC and a whole bunch of other
lines.  There are standard NMEA commands which let you turn off everything
except for GPRMC.  I had to do this the other day to ease the text
processing burden on an 8-bit based system I am working on which only
needs time and location information.  Have a look at the PSRF103 command
to disable all but GPRMC.  A little C based GPRMC parser is all you want
and will be no more effort (probably less) than trying to install and
interface to GPSD.

If your time accuracy requirement is only to the second and not the milli
or microsecond then GPRMC by itself is fine, otherwise I'd look into using
the PPS signal that many (but not all) GPS units provide as well.
