Diego Berge wrote:
>    What I don't like about the idea of detecting modems is that I don't
> see how that can be useful to gpsd, other than deciding that it's
> possibly not a GPS device (unless you are dealing with a Thuraya phone,
> which will respond to an "AT\r" as a modem, but will also give you its
> GPS position given the right AT command).  Detecting what specific
> device is on the serial port belongs somewhere else.


I remember some years ago working with an embedded CDMA UDP|TCP/IP device
the outer level serial command set was with a series of AT commands.
Depending on your POV either the authors decided to be cute and reuse it
or decided not to reinvent the wheel, but either way it was fairly natural
to code for. None the less Tx:AT Rx:OK  from gpsd would prove this thing
wasn't a GPS it knew how to talk to, but your Thuraya phone example reminds
me that there is a possibility that it could potentially be.
