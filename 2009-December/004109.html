Thanks for this Gary.

Appears to be a common enough complaint for this model. After a good deal more searching i found on the globalsat forum a solution. http://www.usglobalsat.com/forum/index.php?topic=911.0

Stop gpsd, start it with

gpsd -NnD4 /dev/gps0

then in another terminal

nc localhost 2947
n0

at this point it still reports N=1 (ie binary)

however remove the device and its now in nmea mode. Led functions again etc.

This method did work for me im glad to say, whereas the proprietary tools that are stated as being capable of changing the mode in these devices both failed. (sirfdemo and sirftech). Its apparently something to do with the the fact that the globalsat devices dont like having both protocol and baud rate changed at the same time, which these tools do. Sirf binary runs at 57600 whereas nmea at 9600.

The other advice from globalsat was to unplug the device for 72 hours. This also did not work for me as it had been unpluged for about 2 weeks, and started in binary first pop.

I think the moral of the story is that for the globalsat devices beware letting gpsd or any of its clients put them into sirf binary, as they can be hard to get out again, and the vast majority of gps softwares dont recognise sirf binary, so nmea is it.
