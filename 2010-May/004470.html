Friends,

To my following questions I got three replies.  One was posted 
on this mailing list.  They were all very informative.  Here I 
post the summary.  I want to share the knowledge with friends.  

Though I do not name you here, thank you very much for valuable 
suggestions!

>(1) NTP ON/OFF behavior
>When the NTP reference is gpsd SHM(0) and SHM(1) only, 
>the synchronization is ON/OFF frequently.

>From replies I learned that "This behavior is normal." and 
"Three or more references are necessary for ntpd to work best."
I my case I am using NMEA (=SHM(0)) and PPT (=SHM(1)) from gpsd.  
Then I may need one more.

Reading "ntpq -p" outputs under this situation, still offset/jitter 
of NMEA is less than 100 and those of PPT is much less.
It won't go further away.  I should be satisfied with this result.

>(2) NTP start problem
>At the same condition (NTP only with gpsd), NTP synchronization 
>does not start if the PC clock is several minutes (maybe) or 
>further off the correct timing...

This is also known behavior of ntpd.  Manual says if the offset 
is more than 1000s then the time synchronize won't start.  I got 
another suggestion that the maximum initial time offset is 180s.  
This number sounds close to my limited experience (not on measurements, 
sorry).  I learned that we can use "ntpd -g" option.  By this option, 
ntpd ignores the initial-offset limitation.

On the time resetting based on the gps-clock reading, I got a 
sugession that the hardware clock should be set at the same 
time.  "hwclock" can be used after the PC clock reset.
Thanks for the sugession.  I will do so.
