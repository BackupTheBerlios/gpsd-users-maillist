assumptions which may hold true for specific uses have a very bad habit
of being abused/applied to the general case further down the track. e.g.
say like when someone plugs in a better class of GPS and expects the
device to give a better class of answer, or the code gets reused for
something else. Also it is worth noting that distortion is cumulative or
hyperbolic as you move away from the origin, not random or static noise.

point to updating the display at more than 30Hz. a few dozen extra cpu
cycles at those frequencies to use the real algorithms ain't a hill of
beans... aka it's still relatively inexpensive even for huge datasets.
