That is a very interesting and well-presented how-to.  If you don't
mind, I'm going to link to it in our recipes section.

Some notes and questions:

1.  Your list of prerequisite packages is somewhat out of date.  
Recent gpsd releases no longer require Motif, but do recommend
Python 2.6.

2.  You write "This option prevents gpsd from writing anything to the
device. In this case it is used to prevent it from shortening the
pulse length of the LVC model from its default of 200 ms to a mere 40
ms."  Where in the code is the pulse width narrowed?  Do you know
why it is narrowed?  Should we remove it?

3. You write "The device is simply not capable of sending its data any
faster, so the objective here is to make it send less data."  Would
bumping the baud rate up to, say, 19200, be a workable alternative?

I ask this not because I think it's a better idea - if the GPS is
being used in stationary mode as a time oracle there's no reason not
to cut its reporting volume - but because knowing that raising the baud rate 
*will* enable 1-per-secoond reports will enrich the reader's mental
model of what is going on.
