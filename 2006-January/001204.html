It's amazing what some pretty pictures can inspire... I've now spent 
a bit too much of the past 24 hours compiling, testing, changing, 
tweaking, compiling and testing again, and now have a "GPS to Google 
Earth" converter that I think is well worth sharing (if only so that 
the alpha geeks among us can *&laugh at the way I've handled what are 
probably simple problems for experienced programmers).

It does pretty much everything I want it to do now, but of course it 
relies on you already having all the scenery in your Google Earth 
cache if you intend to go out and drive around using it. 
Alternatively, it could be fun to use in conjunction with gpspipe 
(while moving around) then gpsfake (to relive the experience with an 
internet-connected Google Earth).

Which brings me to my two questions:

1) how is it best to share my code with gpsd users (and indeed, is 
anyone other than me interested in this?)?

2) how can I use gpsfake to play back logfiles I've dumped with 
gpspipe?  Every time I try it, I get the following result:

Traceback (most recent call last):
   File "/usr/bin/gpsfake", line 10, in ?
     import gpsfake
ImportError: No module named gpsfake

(running on Mac OS X 10.4.3)


And now, I'm going to go driving around to see how gpstoge works.  Of 
course, I'll keep my eyes on the real road rather than the virtual
