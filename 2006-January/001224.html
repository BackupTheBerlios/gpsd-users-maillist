Robin,
> for people to post the IP addresses of their internet-accessible
gpsds?

I think what you want is the capability found in the FriendsD daemon
(UDP) or my gpsd-tracker(http).  Some, people report their position to
the server and you can get the data.  I wrote a CGI Google Maps app to
view the friendsD data and I just put it on the wiki if you want to
see.

I personally wouldn't host a gpsd server for the general Internet
because it won't close the connections like you'd want on the net. 
Also, r and w options would kill your bandwidth.  If there was a way to
easily hack the daemon to close request after one request and only
support the OSDPTVAE requests (others?), then maybe.  Also,is there a
way to feed a lat lon without a GPS it self, no need for one when
you're sitting still.

> I'll try to update the version on Michael Davis's 
> wiki shortly.  

You can upload a new version using the same filename.  If you click on
the "action" link beside your c program and just upload the new file
the wiki will keep the revisions for you.
