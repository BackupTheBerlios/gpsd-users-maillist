On Sun, 15 Jan 2006, sowmya menon wrote:

>   I am using bu-303 usb 1.1 gps receiver.
>   Can someone please send me the linux drivers?

I do not have one of those units, but in the interst of a quick reply to
your question I do not believe any driver is needed for that unit.  Linux
just sees it as a new serial port.

Do this to se that Linux sees your unit:

# cat /proc/bus/usb/devices

Run "dmesg" just after you conect the device and the output should tell
you if the GPS was detected and what serial device it was assiged to.

You might see a line similar to this:
	usb 2-1: Garmin GPS usb/tty converter now attached to ttyUSB0

That tells you your GPS is a /dve/ttyUSB0.  Then just try this on the
command line:
	gpsd -nN -D 5 /dev/ttyUSB0

Watch the debugging output and see if your GPS is detected.  Be sure to use
a recen gpsd build, like 2.30.  THings should jsut work for you.
