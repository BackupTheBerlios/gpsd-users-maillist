>I personally wouldn't host a gpsd server for the general Internet
>because it won't close the connections like you'd want on the net.

Actually, gpsd will close any client connection after 15 minutes of 
inactivity, and I think substantially less in some cases (3 minutes 
for "NOREAD_TIMEOUT", for example)... so unless someone creates the 
world's most popular GPSD server, that should be OK.

>Also, r and w options would kill your bandwidth.  If there was a way to
>easily hack the daemon to close request after one request and only
>support the OSDPTVAE requests (others?), then maybe.  Also,is there a
>way to feed a lat lon without a GPS it self, no need for one when
>you're sitting still.

It'd be pretty simple to edit or comment out blocks of the 
request-handling switch() in gpsd.c to disable specific commands 
(such as R and W)... although again, unless there were many 
simultaneous clients, even a few Rs wouldn't be too much of a problem 
(a simple calculation says it would take at least 7 raw feeds of a 
4800 baud NMEA stream [which in turn would have to be running at 
maximum capacity] to fill the bandwidth of a 33.6k modem).  That 
said, my gpstoge program is very bandwidth-friendly: one O command 
every three seconds.
