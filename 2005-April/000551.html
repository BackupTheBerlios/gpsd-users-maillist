Here's some further testing.....

If I have the GPS in NMEA mode, gpsd WILL recognize it as a generic  
NMEA GPS (see output at bottom).  It should probably be added to the  
compatible list, as NMEA mode only, for now.

If I try sirfmon, it auto-bauds at 4800, shows the ncurses screen, and  
then appears to hang.  I think sirfmon is trying to put the GPS into  
SiRF mode.  Also, the Leadtek SiRF utility recognizes the unit when in  
NMEA mode, but will not change it to SiRF mode.....  After running  
sirfmon or the Leadtek SiRF utility (trying to change it to SiRF mode),  
the GPS gets "really confused".  It just appears to put out a random  
string of 0xF8's & 0x80's (I can supply if anyone's interested).   
NOTHING (sirfmon, gpsd, GlobalSAT's supplied utility, Leadtek's Palm  
Pilot utility, SiRFDemo.exe for windows)  will recognize it, until I  
remove power (AC and Battery), for several minutes.  This puts the GPS  
back into NMEA mode at 4800 baud.  Evidently, before I first tried it  
with gpsd, I got it "really confused" with sirfmon, or some other tool.  
  The thing is, I though I had it working with the Leadtek Palm Pilot  
utility, in SiRF mode.....  I'm going to do some more testing with the  
Leadtek Palm Pilot utility this afternoon.

The utilities that came with the GPS are windows/pocket pc only.  When  
the GPS is "really confused", even these (at least the PC utility - I  
don't have access to a pocket pc) utilities won't recognize it.  I  
think they are expecting NMEA mode.

I'm searching for more information.  GlobalSAT supplies two (2), pretty  
generic, PDF's as documentation.  There's not even a piece of paper in  
the box the GPS came in!

If anyone has any tools/ideas/code to hack on, to get the unit into  
SiRF mode, please let me know.
