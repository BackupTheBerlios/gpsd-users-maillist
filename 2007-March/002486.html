On 3/1/07, Paul Bawon <alkemiman at yahoo.co.uk> wrote:
> Hi,
>
> I'm building a pedestrian based GPS application that uses GPSD to
> pull the data from the GPS unit and pass it to my application. I have
> noticed that, especially in urban canyons, the positions jumps around
> a lot.
>
> I was wondering whether anyone was aware of, or if there are any
> plans to include, a algorithm that filters the data based on physical
> improbability of a pedestrian being able to move that fast, and
> therefore dampens this movement down?
>
> Any help would be greatly appreciated.

GPSD just passes on what the receiver gives us.

Some receivers (ie. SiRFstar 2 and 3) have static navigation and DOP
filter modes. if you're not moving they just hold your position, and
if constellation geometry is too poor they don't output a fix.

Trimble receivers have a configurable dynamics mode ...  land, sea or airborne.

uBlox takes that further: their dynamics modes are: Stationary,
Pedestrian, Automotive, Sea Airborne with (<1g, <2g, <4g) Acceleration

GPSBabel has a number of filters. I believe they can exclude points
based on DOP or other criteria. You may want to check that out.
