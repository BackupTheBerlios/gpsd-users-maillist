Massimo Burcheri <burcheri at fgan.de>:
> But then actually I don't see the point why to use gpsd? Could you tell
> me? The device is currently connected by a RS232-TCP-Gateway and gpsd
> runs locally. Writing my own interpreter I could also use NMEA from
> serial port.
> Could you exemplify the purpose of gpsd for that case?

The NMEA standard is underspecified. and actual NMEA varies all over the lot.
It's quite difficult to write an interpreter for NMEA that is stable and robust
in the presence of all the sludge different GPS receivers emit.  

Trust me on this, as I've been maintaining an NMEA parser for two years now -- 
you really don't want to go there :-(.

It would be more useful, I suspect, to run the gpsd under gdb and localize
those segfaults.
