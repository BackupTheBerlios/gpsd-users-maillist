We welcome suggestions on how to improve the doc.  It does assume a
fair knowledge of how NTP works.

> localhost ntp # ntpq -p
>     remote           refid      st t when poll reach   delay   offset  jitter
> ==============================================================================
> +ntp1.sp.se      .PPS.            1 u   51   64  377   25.794    1.523   0.193
> xSHM(0)          .GPS.            0 l    3   16  377    0.000  445.125  25.923
> *SHM(1)          .GPS1.           0 l    4   16  377    0.000   -0.006   0.001

That is looking much better.  After a few days it will pull in even more.

> I just followed the example of ntp.conf  with "time1 0.420" offset,
> how do I determine the real offset for _my_ device?

As the ntpq shows, you are locked on to SHM(1) which is your gpsd/PPS. That
is  about 1mS from ntp1.sp.se.  Unless you have been running on PPS for
several days and you are really close to ntp1.sp.se, I would just leave
that alone.

You can also see that SHM(0) which is your TSIP decoded time is off about
445mS.  First I would see how "time1 0" works for you and adjust until
you are happy.  Given the jitter on the serial time you can not expect to
dial it in better than about +/- 200mS.
