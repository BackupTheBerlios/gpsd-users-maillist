Ryan Turcotte 10E wrote:
> Hi all,
>
> I've discovered USB GPS dongles are pretty terrible for usage with ntpd for any sort of time sync. It's not the delay, it's too much random jitter. The offset spans the range of -250ms to +250ms

You will get jitter with these because of the lack of a decent antenna and the height above the 
ground.  If you go to a different make & model of GPS with the same antenna issues you'll just 
have the same problem.

I have one external USB GPS that gives me a lot of position and time jitter, but another one 
has an antenna jack to which I've cabled a length of co-ax cable running to a dedicated Garmin 
GPS antenna at the back of the boat.  That gives me much better accuracy (my boat is not 
"inland" quite so often according to the traces) and much less jitter.

One common misconception / fault is to place the GPS antenna as high as possible.  This is 
wrong, and causes signal bounce from the ground / water, which causes the GPS to mistake its 
position and time signal.  The correct location for the GPS antenna is on the gunwale rail or 
pushpit rail, close to the water & as far from the mast as possible (to reduce signal bounce 
from the mast).  If you're in a car, don't put the GPS antenna on the roof, put it on the 
towbar or similar.  If you're outside or in a fixed location, put the GPS antenna as far from 
buildings as possible, and on the ground.  Accept the fact that if you're driving in a heavily 
built up area you're going to get signal bounce and lack of accuracy.  My in car GPS beeped at 
me last week because it thought I was travelling at 15000 km/hr.
