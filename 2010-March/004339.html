2010/3/28 Håkan Johansson <f96hajo at chalmers.se>

>
> Hi Greg,
>
> 10 µs _should_ be fine.  In addition to long pulses (say a few hundred µs
> up, but there is really no hard limit), also short pulses (like just 1 µs
> long from sirfstarIII, e.g. from MR-350P or EM406A) are detected.  There is
> a chance of pulses in the region a few µs to ~a hundred µs would interfer
> badly with the correct pulse-edge detection --- I'm not aware of this being
> tested yet.
>
> It would thus be interesting if you could provide a log from a run with 10
> µs pulses.  E.g. with
>
> gpsd -N -n /dev/tty... -D 6 2>&1 | grep -E "NTPD|PPS"
>
> and data from some 10 s of running or so
>
> My personal guess is that 10 µs will go by the short-pulse method
> (invisible pulse detected), while the breakpoint is at 20-40 µs depending on
> computer speed (latency from interrupt to user-space).
>
> Cheers,
> Håkan
