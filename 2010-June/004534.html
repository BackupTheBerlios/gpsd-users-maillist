Derek C <derekgpsd at hssl.ie>:
> This query is perhaps a bit off-topic because it looks, to me, like the
> problem is the GPS units I'm trying (two different types) rather than gpsd
> itself.
> 
> I'm using gpsd in a project (OpenWRT Kamikaze 8.09.2 on a PC Engines ALIX
> board).
> 
> I see a funny problem that occurs after the unit is on for a couple, or a
> few, days:  gpsd returns no lock when I telnet and send the "p" command.

You're running an old-protocol daemon, then.  "P" is no longer supported.
You should upgrade, though I don't think this has anything to do with your
problem.
 
> I also notice that the raw output from the /dev/ttyUSBX gps device seems
> to have a lot of commas in the GPGSA line when the problem is happening.

GPGSA has a fixed field count.  The lots of adjacent commas just
means the GPS doesn't think it sees signal from most of the satellites
in the skyview.
 
> When I reboot the unit (a "reboot" command works fine) gpsd starts return
> the co-ordinates again in response to a "p" command.

It looks as though what's happening is that the GPS firmware is dropping 
all but the three strongest channels, and noyt trying to reacquire until
you reboot.

> Has anyone else comes across this?  Is there any way to tell the usb gps
> units to fix themselves without the reboot?

Sorry, I don't know of one.
