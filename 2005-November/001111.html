just to note that any time you do this sort of thing with a RF cable you
will probably cut your signal strengh by a factor 2.

If you can get your hands on a $300+ TNC crimp tool and terminate the
connection properly you won't lose as much, but still will lose.

also slightly off-thread but of interest, I have just written a module
for GRASS GIS called "d.out.gpsdrive" for easily making GpsDrive
backdrop maps. It exports the contents of the GIS display as a
registered background image all ready to go, just restart GpsDrive(?).
GRASS (via GDAL/OGR) will let you import & reproject just about any
spatial data format out there & plot it up using SQL queries, custom
color tables, grid overlays, logos, aligned street name text, etc.

An example use: with a predetermined grid of gpsdrive image tiles and
gpsd running in the background you can do real-time and dynamic shortest
route analysis of a road network with GRASS's v.net.* and d.path modules
and have it "appear beneath your feet" as you move along. Viva GNU.
