On Wed, 23 Nov 2005, James Burns wrote:

> I'd be happy to do the testing. I think that on these models with the
> binary protocol you get 1 second fixes rather than 2 second fixes
> helping with accuracy as well as jitter / latency. That's what I'm
> most interested in as I use the gps for wardriving and fixes every 2
> seconds at 60 mph is not good. Let me know when you've got something
> you'd like to test and I'll test it out. Thanks for your help.

The garmin binary will not improve things very much.  You will get almost
as good results by setting the serial speed to the fastest possible.  Or
get the Garmin GPS 18/USB for the same price.

Try the fudge and see how much of the fixed drift you can get rid of.
For reasons lost in history ther is also a fudge added to the NMEA time
in gpsd.  So just play with it and see how much is left.  Then be sure
all unneeded NMEA sentences are turned off.  My guess is you will end
up around 300mS variable.  With the GPS 18/USB I see around 30mS jitter.
