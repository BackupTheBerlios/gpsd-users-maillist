On Thu, 24 Nov 2005, Darryl Ross wrote:

> As you can see, the offset for the SHM source is 0.5 (seconds?). That seems to
> vary a bit, but that would be about the average I think.

Yes, the 0.5 seconds is delay in the system from the time the GPS grabs
the time until it winds its way around and around and out to your ntp
daemon.  You can use the "fugde" value in the ntp.conf to remove the
fixed part of the error.  There will still be quite a lot of jitter
left due to the NMEA and serial connection.  Not all NMEA sentences are
output on every cycle and they vary in length a lot.  Be happy that you
are within a second of true time.

> If it makes any difference the GPS is in NMEA mode. In SiRF mode the offset
> seemed to be about 13s.

13 is the current number of leap seconds.  Next year it will be 14.  Somebody
is forgetting the leap seconds.  SiRF is not my thing so I can't help
there.
