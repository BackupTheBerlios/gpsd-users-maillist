Matthias Urlichs <smurf at smurf.noris.de>:
> I might be opening a can of worms here, but why not use the dbus
> protocol as a base for GPSD-NG?

A non-textual command/response format, limited to local use, with
endianness issues, and critically dependent on a complex and poorly
documented codebase?  Absolutely no way.  Bad idea on *many* levels.
Ignores every Unix/Internet lesson about good protocol design.

There are large advantages to having a command language that can be
eyeballed and typed by humans, and for which test jigs are easy to 
build in any scripting language or with cat, grep, sed, and awk.

Not that I'm discouraging you from improving the D-Bus reporting code
we already have.  Using that to broadcast fixes and status information
locally on D-Bus-capable systems totally makes sense.  But not as a
primary command language.
