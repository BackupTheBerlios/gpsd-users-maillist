Eric Fowler <eric.fowler at gmail.com>:
> Well, the standard is in the public domain, sort of: the Coast Guard
> publishes what the VHF transcievers are expected to send/receive over the
> airwaves, but they don't care about anything below the tip of the antenna -
> so NMEA is free to put on their own private "standard" for the strings the
> receivers put out on rs232.
> 
> It looks like the NMEA "sentences" are just header, some data from the
> over-air data packet, over-air packet encoded as ASCII, checksum, CRLF.

That's interesting, especially if we can find the Coast Guard standard 
describing the radio packets - because I think we already know how to
read the encoding on those payload blocks in AIVDM.  From two different
sources, the Bosun's Mate page and gnuais.

I'd *love* to put a page on the GPSD site describing the AIVDM format
as "here are the bits in the radio packets" and "here's how those bits
get turned into AIVDM/NMEA packets.  With a nice big 'graph at the top
saying:  "You don't have to buy ITU Recommendation M.1371 and swallow their
evil licensing terms, here's everything you need to know..."
