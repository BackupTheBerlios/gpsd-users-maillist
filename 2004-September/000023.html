As I understand it, DOP is unit-less, and can only be compared 
meaningfully to other DOP figures.  A DOP of 4 indicates twice the 
likelihood of a given position error compared with a DOP of 2.  The 
DOP is calculated from the expected errors due to current geometry of 
the satellites used to obtain the fix.  The estimated position errors 
should show a strong correlation with DOP, but be completely 
different in value as they are measured in distance units (i.e. 
metres), and they are trying to tell you "you're very probably within 
x metres of this point" rather than "I'm about twice as sure of my 
position as I was a couple of  minutes ago".
