Hi,
I don't have a GPS unit, and never heard of gpsd before, but it came
preinstalled with the latest Fedora release, and every time that I plug
in a serial to USB adapter gpsd sends some data as if there were a GPS
on the other end of the cable.

It took me some time to figure out what was happening. I use serial
ports for microcontroller projects, and this data seems to cause issues
with bootloaders. At first I thought it could be a bug in the kernel
driver for USB/serial adapters, but then a look at

tail -f /var/log/messages

reported something related to gpsd. So I tried to see if there was some
traffic on the serial port immediately after connecting the USB/serial
adapter, and logged it in the file screenlog.txt that is attached to
this email.

I have tried two different USB/serial adapters:

ID 0403:6001 Future Technology Devices International, Ltd FT232
USB-Serial (UART) IC
ID 067b:2303 Prolific Technology, Inc. PL2303 Serial Port

and both trigger gpsd into thinking it's a GPS unit.
At first I tried to simply "yum remove gpsd" but the whole KDE
environment seems to depend on it...

So, I'm asking if it is possible to disable gpsd without uninstalling it
(I'm not an expert of udev).

And lastly, despite I've read http://esr.ibiblio.org/?p=801 and
discovered that dealing with GPSes is a mess, I still think that opening
automatically every USB/serial adapter and start sending data is a bit
invasive.
