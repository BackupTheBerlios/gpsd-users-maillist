> 
> I am running a Fedora13 system using GPSD 2.39. Contrary to what I
> have seen elsewhere, 2.39 uses the OLD interface format. The gps
> receiver is a bu-353.
> 
> I have several programs attaching to gpsd and getting output. In
> fact, I have actually made the FireFox gpsd interface work. Which
> brings me to my question.
> 
> The man page for gpsd defines the "o" output as having a "tag",
> defined as:
> 
>      "A tag identifying the last sentence received. For NMEA devices 
> this is just
>        the NMEA sentence name; the talker-ID portion may be useful for
>         distinguishing among results produced by different NMEA
> talkers in the same
>         wire."
> 
> On my system, gpsd is sending back "o" messages with a "tag" of
> "GND". Given that this is supposed to be an NMEA sentence name, two
> things are in question. 1)Is the bu-353 an NMEA device, and 2) what
> NMEA sentence is being mapped?

The BU-353 is a SiRF device (http://gpsd.berlios.de/hardware.html), and
is capable of both NMEA and SiRF binary. As noted at
http://gpsd.berlios.de/hacking.html#autoconfiguration, if gpsd
detects a SiRF receiver, gpsd will switch to SiRF, firmware version
permitting. So I don't know what is going on with the "o" messages.
