Yo Ryan!

On Tue, 16 Feb 2010, Ryan Turcotte 10E wrote:

> Silly of ntpd
> to ignore gpsd data when the time is that far off, IMO.

ntpd, by default, ignores time sources that are more  than 1000s off at
startup.  They decided better to have no time than a badtime.

You can use the -g option to allow the first bump to be be anything
goes, but as you see that does not seem to work well with gpsd.  Not sure
why that is the case.  There  have been NTP  tweaks in gpsd since 2.90 so
a later version might work a bit better for you.

gpsd has been known to go wonky so best to also have other time sources
available and to let ntpd sort out the best of them.

For example, the SiRF leap second detection in current gpsd will just
go off int he weedsand best not to have ntpd follow that!
