On Tue, 2011-03-08 at 22:28 -0700, Charles Curley wrote:

> Right. But a client programmer could call it in a loop. On Linux.

Or, even less obtrusive to the program, use SIGIO on the file
descriptor. Then your handler is only called when there is something to
read. No sloppy polling or looping. Of course you need to arrange for
making the data available to your program via a signal handler. But
there are textbook solutions for that. Personally, we set the GPS
blocking and then do a blocking read in a thread. Effectively the same
as the SIGIO solution.
