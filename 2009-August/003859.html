On Sun, 9 Aug 2009, Ekki Plicht (DF4OR) wrote:
> I have the need for a copy of the serial data (in NMEA format) of the
> GPS device.  Besides the computer use of the GPS data I have a second
> device which would like to be fed with NMEA data (a radio, like in Ham
> Radio and APRS).

As long as the ham radio never wants to *talk* to the GPS receiver, you
could just run another wire to the serial port on the ham radio.  RS-232
isn't officially supposed to work this way, but as long as you aren't
trying to drive (say) 10 devices from one device, it usually works OK.

You end up with a cable with three connectors:

GPS         PC with    Ham
receiver    gpsd       radio
RX  <-------< TX

TX  >---+---> RX
         |
         '--------------> RX

Gnd ----+---- Gnd
         |
         '--------------- Gnd

You would have to tell gpsd to stay in NMEA mode, and make sure that
the GPS receiver is configured (either in its non-volatile memory or by
gpsd) to use the same serial line settings (eg 4800 8N1) and to emit all
the NMEA sentences that the ham radio expects to see.

Again, don't land airplanes with this.  But for just messing around, it
should be fine.
