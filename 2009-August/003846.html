> Now I understand why this doesn't work.
> 
> The gpsd daemon is designed to be hooked up to live sensors. 
> Wired deep into its architecture is the assumption that any
> given sensor connection has only one class of data it will
> emit during its lifetime (e.g. from first packet lock to
> decvice close).
> 
> Is this assumption invalid?  Are there devices (or device
> configurations) that really intermix both AIS and GPS sentences?

It is quite common on boats that multiple NMEA inputs sent to the
autopilot are combined and reoutputted (now mixed + derivatives
added [true wind speed from measured apparent wind speed]) to external
devices/displays.

typically this would be depth sounder and wind data but it is highly
plausible that AIS would be treated the same.

I've got a fair bit of mixed GPS/sounder NMEA logs if it would be
useful to someone. (note these are still NMEA '$aabbb,' format)
