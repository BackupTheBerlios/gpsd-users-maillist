>    I'm not able to test my theory now, but if I understand correctly
> what you're trying to achieve, would that not be feasible by starting
> just one gpsd and two clients?
> 
>    That's the approach I used on a xgps-like client (qtgpsc).  It opens
> one connection for the data displayed on the UI widgets, and a second
> connection to the same server if the user requests a "raw data" feed
> (where raw can be binary, NMEA, or GPSD format).
> 
>    I imagine you've probably thought of this already, but FWIW.
> 

No that wouldn't work. There is no option to force the gpsd to send data
of a specific type. gpsd uses its own logic to decide what to send. You
can only choose between raw and cooked. If you choose cooked than the
gpsd logic will take over and in the end will only send AIS sentences.

If there was a way to force the logic to select af specific driver than
it would work. That is what I expected from:

?WATCH={"TPV":true}

to give me the gps sentences 

and 
?WATCH={"AIS":true}

to give me the AIS sentences

I even expected

?WATCH={"TPV":true,"AIS":true}

to give me both sentences on one connection
