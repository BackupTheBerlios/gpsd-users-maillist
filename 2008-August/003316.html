On Thu, 14 Aug 2008, Ulrich Goebel wrote:
> I would have to put a Bluetooth-Device in the USB-port and then connect
> the GPS-Mouse via this Bluetooth. Does that work? Could anyone give me
> some hints how to configure gpsd to make that work?

When you connect the Bluetooth receiver to the USB port, the kernel
should assign it a device name, usually something like /dev/ttyFOO .
This may take a second or two.  Under Linux, running the 'dmesg'
command after you connect the Bluetooth receiver will probably show you
the device name.  (Note that the dmesg output may only show you the part
of the name that starts with "tty".)  You can then give this device name
as an argument to gpsd.

For example, if dmesg says something like "Bluetooth receiver attached
as ttyBLU1", you would say

gpsd /dev/ttyBLU1

to start gpsd.  You may also want to use the -b switch to prevent gpsd
from attempting to adjust the baud rate of the GPS receiver, which is
known to break some Bluetooth GPSes:

gpsd -b /dev/ttyBLU1
