Ugh... I've not seen the NMEA sentence itself, but by observation
of ntp setup with the et-102 in $GPGGA sentence only mode (done with a
hacked sirfmon) I've seen ntpd show the 1 second offset sloshing
back and forth, just like you reported with your ntpd setup.

To be precise, I used the hacked sirfmon to put the et-102 into
$GPGGA sentence only mode (ntpd supports this sentence as
"mode 2") so that there was minimum extraneous serial data
being delivered. I verified that only this sentence was being
emitted by the et-102. I then ran the et-102 with ntpd on both the
linuxpps and freebsd machines using the ntpd NMEA driver. 
Both setups saw the slop. 

The gpsd was not used for that experiment.
