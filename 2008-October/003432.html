OS isn't quite as big a problem as you'd think. every unix-like OS
i've seen has drivers for FTDI, Prolific, SiLabs and ACM usb devices.
Those are by far the most common devices you'll see. oh, and
bluetooth. There are some devices using a Cypress chip, and garmin usb
devices are a whole 'nother kind of evil...

We don't bother collecting OS since it's not really our problem -
we're a layer or two up the stack. We trust the OS to hand us
something that looks a lot like a tty that gratuitously emits byte
that strongly resemble some well-known formats.

While vendors may change the BOM depending on which of FTDI, PL or SL
are cheaper this week, they tend not to. They find a reference design
and stick with it. Also over the years that I've been supporting gpsd,
pretty much every instance of an unsupported USB device has been
traced back to not having the kernel module loaded. If a device was
particularly difficult to get working, we'd mention that in the notes
column, or give it a lower compatibility rating.

That being said, you would probably find some decent receivers if you
search for ones with OSX or linux compatibility.
