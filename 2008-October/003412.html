On Tue, Oct 7, 2008 at 10:58 PM, Hamish <hamish_b at yahoo.com> wrote:
> But we know that the almanac changes, and thus it is guaranteed that at
> least some of the time packet != packet+1, and so you can't just graft
> the blobs of two of them together and assume you have a valid packet.
>
> e.g. if a new satellite is added does the length of the almanac grow or
> shift some number of bits, or otherwise allow a graft to get corrupted?

The almanac size is constant. The parts of the almanac that you can't
just glom together are protected by an Issue-Of-Data counter. There
are serveral. You can and do get different IOD's for different PRN's
- that's allowed. The IOD is there to help you tell when that PRN has
been updated.

> I assume the thing has lots of checksums in it which may help, and
> if someone was smart perhaps it even sends a packet ID at both the start
> and end of each almanac, but I have no idea about the binary internals of
> it.

The checksum is more to protect the signal-in-space than to ensure
internal consistency.
