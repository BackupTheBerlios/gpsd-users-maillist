Correct.


the GARMIN protocol (as normally used) is for transfering waypoints,
tracks and routes to and from the GPS.

e.g. use with gpstrans, gpsbabel softwares.

There are other novel uses of it though-

"The Garmin G12, 12XL, II+, III+, ETREX, EMAP, and G76 provide
pseudorange and carrier cycle information in an asynchronous data format
when correctly queried via the serial port in the GARMIN data protocol. 
Indications are that Doppler information is also available."
  http://gpsinformation.net/pseudorange.htm

(i.e. DGPS from a cheap handheld unit, after post-processing)
