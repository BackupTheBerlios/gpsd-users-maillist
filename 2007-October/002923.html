Hiya,

I've encountered this also.

If you have a 'slow' PC (I encountered this on a PIII 1GHz), I found that 
the act of turning on the -D5 debugging with the -N option was enough to 
break PPS. I can only put it down to the time taken to output the debug 
data to console was enough to break the PPS timing loop.

Try running GPSD over a period of time without  -D5 and use ntpq to see if 
the PPS pulse is eventually recognised by ntpd.
