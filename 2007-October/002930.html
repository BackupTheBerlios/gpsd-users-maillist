That's interesting.  The unit I am using is being run on an industrial  
PentiumMMX that uses the TSC clock (as expected) for high 
accuracy.  "ntpq -p" shows that the PPS signal can drift out by hundreds of 
microseconds upon which ntp prefers using the stratum 12 local clock.  After 
a while ntp will lock onto the PPS signal again before it drifts away.  I 
haven't tested the unit on faster hardware so I can't blame anything in 
particular for this behaviour.  The Pentium is asleep most of the time so I 
doubt it's stressed keeping track of the time with ntpd and gpsd.

I get NTP accuracy in the sub millisecond range anyway which is fine for my 
Windows networking purposes.
