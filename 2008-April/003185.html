Have a look at some of the sample clients such as
http://svn.berlios.de/svnroot/repos/gpsd/trunk/cgpxlogger.c - this is
a fairly simple client program to connect to gpsd and record your
track. It contains basically the bare minimum required to connect to
gpsd and process responses.

The mask is just a collection of bits that describe what quantities
are available or valid in the fix structure. Let's say you only have a
2D fix. The altitude member of the structure will contain a value ...
maybe your last altitude, maybe 0.0, maybe NaN... but since it's 2D
the ALTITUDE_SET mask bit won't be set so you just ignore that member
entirely - it's full of lies. There are a number of other mask bits
available (check the headers).
