Well I am delighted to say I have gpsd feeding multiple programs quite
happily here, and that's really what I want it for, but it could be
easier.

To feed mpfcNavigator (which will only talk to a device file) I am
having to run:

	socat -dd tcp4:localhost:2947 PTY:link=/tmp/navigatorgps &
	echo r1 >> /tmp/navigatorgps

And to feed a windows based chart plotter in a VMWware session, I get
VMWare to setup a PTY as master in /tmp and again use socat to feed it
data:

	socat -dd tcp4:localhost:2947,retry unix-connect:/tmp/vmware-com1

This time I have to go to hyperterm to feed an "r" to get raw data
through.

What I would _like_ is for another option to gpsd that would open
another port (2948?) and automatically feed raw gpsd data to any
connected clients on that port. That would make like a much easier!

But socat is my friend...

(I did try using 'gloo' to feed the r1 in... but did not get very far -
kept creating loops apparently.)
