> Hello I am new to gps development. I am writing an application for my
> school where the user tries to survey using gps. I have a Garmin etrex
> unit with me to test code. The user takes the hand-held unit and walks
> around while the gps unit saves the point every 10 seconds. He then
> saves the points into a track. My question is can the NMEA protocol
> get a track that was saved in the unit? I didn't see this capability
> but I might be mistaken. The reason is that I want the program to work
> with as many gps units as possible. Thanks in advance for your help.

gpsd is really for real time data acquisition, not downloading stored
data.

To download track data you need to use the Garmin/Garmin protocol.

If your GPS is smart enough, you might be able to download waypoints via
NMEA, I'm really not sure if the eTrex is designed for that.

For the garmin, try gpstrans for downloading the track (Linux/UNIX/OSX?).
It works well.
  http://gpstrans.sourceforge.net/


If you are stuck using windows, try GPSTrackMaker.
  http://www.gpstm.com/


A step further than that is to use the v.in.garmin module to load your
data directly into GRASS GIS where you can make graphics, do analysis,
export to lots of other formats, etc. (new improved 5.7 version just
added to CVS last week; the help page is missing some info until next
week's rebuild) Have a talk with the folks in the Soil & Water
Laboratory, Dept. of Biological & Environmental Engineering (Cornell)
if you want to see it in action.

5.7 version:
http://grass.ibiblio.org/grass57/manuals/html57_user/v.in.garmin.html

5.3 versions:
http://grass.ibiblio.org/gdp/html_grass5/html/s.in.garmin.sh.html
http://grass.ibiblio.org/gdp/html_grass5/html/v.in.garmin.sh.html
