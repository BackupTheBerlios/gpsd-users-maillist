On 1/15/07, Ali Utku Selen <selenau at kentkart.com.tr> wrote:
> I believe it is not a good idea to use any available sql packages to store
> gps data on the device itself because of "Wear Levelling" issues.

When was the last time you actually wore out a CF card? If you're half
way clever about it, you can probably keep your writes down to 1-4KB
once a minute. That makes for a long time before you've rewritten a
sector long enough to hurt.

I've been running my home firewall on CF for about 5 years. The
filesystem is FFS, it's read-write, and I send my logs to CF because
it's persistent. CF cards have lots of extra sectors to replace worn
ones and their firmware does wear levelling.

Given that a Zaurus has an SD slot and you can get an OCZ 1GB, 150x SD
card for $20 you'd be silly to not use something like that. 2GB, 80x
Lexar CF cards are only $90. They use way less power than a spinning
disk, and they're way cheaper to replace.
