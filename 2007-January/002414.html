Chris Kuethe wrote:

> Maybe if Diego can tell us a little bit more about the expected IO
> patterns... how much, how often, whether there is a lot of write or
> rewrite.

   Not a lot in fact.  I have some code which I'll post to my ftp when
I'm able to, but as a rough description, what I'm talking about is an
application not totally unlike xgps but with a stakeout function.  You
load a full database of points[1] you want staked out and then you
select one of those at a time, walk up to it, press the RECORD key and
it stores an observation, then you move to the next point and the
process is restarted.  You are talking max. 1000 writes per day at a
rate of one every 2-3 min.  With a track logging function (i.e., record
automatically on distance/time) it could be more but still not an
outrageous lot.

   Thanks for your help, btw.  I will look into the options suggested
when I get to somewhere civilised, or at least inhabited :-P

[1] It needn't be points, it could also be a geometry (e.g., a road
centreline) which takes very little storage.  OTOH, if I'm going to
store the design as points (some features do not have a definite
geometry, other than being discrete points), it needs to be a huge
amount of them---100,000 would be the minimum.  Very roughly you are
talking 100Mb all-up.
