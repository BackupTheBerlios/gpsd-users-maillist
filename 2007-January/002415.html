What, no path solver to find the most efficient path to visit all the
points? ;) And what about tetris... I demand you make it play tetris!

> With a track
> logging function (i.e., record automatically on distance/time) it
> could be more but still not an outrageous lot.

track logs are tiny... ~100B for an O message and less than 32B if you
want binary records. You can probably buffer the track log to be
written only when the RECORD key is pressed.

> [1] It needn't be points, it could also be a geometry (e.g., a road
> centreline) which takes very little storage.  OTOH, if I'm going to
> store the design as points (some features do not have a definite
> geometry, other than being discrete points), it needs to be a huge
> amount of them---100,000 would be the minimum.  Very roughly you are
> talking 100Mb all-up.

sqlite would work, assuming you have a sane schema... which is pretty
critical for any database.

there was a commercial navigator product that used sqlite3 as its
datastore, and put about 1.5M records (~1GB) into the database: roads,
"administrative boundaries", points of interest, location names...
Incidentally, that product was also running a 200-300MHz ARM with 64MB
of memory. It also ran gpsd...
