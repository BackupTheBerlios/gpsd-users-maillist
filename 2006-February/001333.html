Yo Andreas!

AFAIK this is in the NMEA 3.0 spec.  Of course I do not have a copy.

Google found this which seems to describe what you are looking for:

	http://www.navcenter.org/marcomms/IEC/80_269e.pdf

See section G.2.8 for the definition and G.3.1 for a fully worked out
decoding example.  The only odd thing is the "six-bit field type".

Looks to me like the marine equivalent of ADS-B.  Ships broadcast
their position to each other and the NMEA sentences allow the data to
be moved around with the other NMEA data.

If gpsd does not pass these sentences along then it could easily be
modified to do so.  Not sure what gpsd could or should do with the data,
except just pass it along.  I guess someone could write a simple decoder
to decode the data after gpsd forwards it.  If you could send us some
raw NMEA we could take a look.
