On Friday 03 February 2006 01:00pm, Gary E. Miller wrote:
> If you can find out what rs-232<->usb chip is in the etrex you could then
> talk to that driver group about grabbing that device in their driver.

Gary, I think you're talking about the chip in the Targus PA088 adapter, no?

Anyway, I could be wrong, but there's a good chance that it's Magic Control 
Technology (based on the 'MCT' reference). They provide chipsets for 
USB/RS-232 converters like the Belkin F5U109/F5U409 (and others). The Linux 
kernel driver that supports these adapters is 'mct_u232'.

Les, can you post the contents of 'cat /proc/bus/usb/devices', please? I'd 
like to see what that Targus adapter looks like to the USB subsystem 
(especially the PID/VID [Product/Vendor IDs]).
