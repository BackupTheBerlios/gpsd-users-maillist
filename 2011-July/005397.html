Just be aware that 1Hz update freq is the norm for GPS.  There exist
3/5/10Hz devices, but they are not the norm and you should investigate
if you get what you think you are getting...

Note that I am led to believe that the gps output for direction vectors
is based on some doppler calculation and is more accurate than you can
calculate using lagged 1Hz position reports (someone please correct this
statement?).  I'm guessing at your requirements, but be aware of this
and also consider using multiple GPS units around your vehicle if the
goal is tracking a high speed vehicle (ie it might not travel in the
direction its facing...).

Also be aware that many GPS units operate at some pitiful data rate like
4,800baud and so the output is lagged significantly over when it's
measured (often multiple hundreds of millisecs). Raising the baud rate
reduces this lag, but it's likely still delayed by a constant over when
it's measured and if you are looking for sub 1Hz accuracy, it's probably
something you need to calibrate?

Good luck
