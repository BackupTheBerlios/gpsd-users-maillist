> gpsd /dev/ttyUSB0

Try

gpsd -D -N 4 /dev/ttyUSB0

to get more debugging information from gpsd.  If
you have some other USB-to-serial device hooked
up, it's entirely possible that your Garmin is on
something other than ttyUSB0.  Wait 10 seconds
or so after plugging in the Garmin, then say

dmesg

to see which ttyUSB it was assigned to.

> I tried to use NetCat
> # nc localhost 2947

Won't work.  gpsd doesn't talk unless you ask it
to.  Use "telnet localhost 2947" and enter some
gpsd commands to see if you get any output.

Also try cgps/xgps, the clients that come with gpsd.
If you are installing from a package manager, you
might have to look for a package called "gpsd-clients"
or similar to get these.
