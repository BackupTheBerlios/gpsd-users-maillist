So where is the actual bug here?  GPSD does not seem to be reporting any
crazy high speeds.

> If you notice the two logs attached you will notice that one started at 
> 4800 and the other started at 9600. These are output of gpsd-2.16 and 
> gpsd-2.14 respectively. The gpsdlog1 (gpsd-2.16) indicated the the sirf 
> driver is used. The gpsdlog-nmea1 (gpsd-2.14) indicates that the nmea 
> driver is used. With gpsd-2.14 you can specify a baudrate. With 
> gpsd-2.16 you cannot so gpsd starts with 4800. I am not sure what is 
> happening.

That part is easy.  Between 2.14 and 2.16 I introduced autobauding and a 
native SiRF driver.  You don't have to specify a baud rate any more because
GPD automatically syncs up to whatever baud rate the GPS is using.  If you
want to change the baud rate, look at the 'B' command.
