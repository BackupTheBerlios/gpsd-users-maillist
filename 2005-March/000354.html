Gene Imes <ebi6 at ozob.net>:
> I have been discussing this with Pascal Martin on the roadmap list and 
> have decided to take it up here on gpsd-users.
> 
> I have a Holux GR-230 bluetooth gps receiver and I connect to it with 
> the mapping program roadmap. I has worked well until recently.
> 
> I went on a trip and the gps tracked well but roadmap indicated I was 
> going approximately 2000 mph. When I am at rest normal gps fix 'jitter' 
> causes the indicated speed to range from 0 to 80 mph which exceeds the 
> threshold in roadmap so that the map rotates around present position 
> about once a second. Bad stuff.
> 
> Until recently I was able to do a 'cat /dev/rfcomm0' and see the output 
> in NMEA but now the output is just garbage or I suspect sirf.
> 
> I am not sure but I think all of this started after an upgrade of gpsd, 
> which I do after every release.
> 
> Is my gps receiver on the fritz or is something else afoot and how do I 
> find out?

I have a guess.  To check it, first run your daemon at -D 4 and see if it
tells you it's switching to SiRF mode. 

If it is, use sirfmon from the source distribution to switch the chip
back to NMEA mode (using the 'n' command), then run the 2.14 version
(that's the last version that doesn't automatically switch to binary
mode when it detects a SiRF chip).

If that solves your problem, then the NMEA mode of your chip is doing some
stutter filtering that is preventing those spurious speed surges -- filtering
that binary mode is not doing.

I doubt this scenario only because I never see speed surges that bad.  I
suspect something else is going on.  But check on it.  If it turns out
this is your problem, I'll implement some way for you to switch out of
binary mode.  Hmmm...maybe I'll do that anyway on general principle.
