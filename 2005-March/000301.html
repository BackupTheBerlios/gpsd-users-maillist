> Does anyone know how bearing or speed is calculated by the gps
> devices? Is it based on the difference between the current and
> previous positions or is there some special calculation going
> at each position calculation?


depends on the device. I know the old garmin handhelds calculate it as a
smoothed running average of distance/time fixes. This is from the days
of selective availability, so the idea was to smooth massive jumps in
position so you were not instantaneously traveling 30m/s from standstill
when the satellites changed.

but again, depends on the device. I'd be surprised if a unit just used
simple difference between two subsequent fixes, but who knows?
