Ok, so I looked further. There are 15 warnings in total. Three are separate 
reports of a single problem in <sys/socket.h> vs <bits/socket.h>, so they're 
presumably of concern to zgcc rather than gpsd (and from a quick look at the 
socket code, I think it does do the right thing - it just doesn't quite 
manage to convince the compiler that it has). The other 12 are in garmin.c as 
follows: line number, and the expression that offended the compiler. Line 
numbers relate to version 2.19 tarball. In each case, the message is 
"warning: cast increases required alignment of target type".

257: (cpo_pvt_data*)pkt->mData
354: (cpo_sat_data*)pkt->mData
571: (Packet_t*)session->GarminBuffer
626: (Packet_t*)buffer
635: (Packet_t*)buffer
662: (Packet_t*)session->GarminBuffer
685: (Packet_t*)buffer
707: (Packet_t*)session->GarminBuffer
731: (Packet_t*)buffer
753: (Packet_t*)session->GarminBuffer
799: (Packet_t*)buffer
817: (Packet_t*)session->GarminBuffer

I assume gpsd hasn't yet been used on an architecture where this matters, but 
I guess it's worth recording.
