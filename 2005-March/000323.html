Yo Rob!


On Fri, 11 Mar 2005, Rob Landley wrote:

> Every actual real world use of GPS I can think of boils down to "tell me
> where I am".

Gotta disagree with you on that one.  That may be what gpsd is for, but
there are lot's of other projects out there, like gpsbabel that do
different things.

Lot's of times I use a standalone GPS where a latptop is just not
an option.  My wife is buying a pedometer that has a GPS in it
so she can get realtime info on her pacing and distance.  She is NOT
gonna carry a laptop, and she knows where she is, just not how fast
or far she is going.

Just off the top of my head, things I have seen in the last 2 weeks:

	1. update the firmware on a GPS.
	2. download the firmware from a GPS before an update, just in case.
	3. upload maps to a emoving map GPS.
	4. upload waypoints and routes to a GPS before a trip.
	5. download breadcrumbs from a GPS after a trip.
        6. upload checklists to a GPS.
        7. use a GPS to get the exact time.

Once again, I am not suggesting gpsd on any of these, but be aware of
other programs that do.
