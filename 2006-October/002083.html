On Sun, 8 Oct 2006, Gian Leonardo Solazzi wrote:

> 2006/10/7, Gary E. Miller <gem at rellim.com>:
> > The -F is for hotplug. Skip that for now until you have the basics
> > working.  Unplug your GPS and then plug it in another USB port.  Then
> > run dmesg and see what the kernel says about the item.
>
> The last rows of dmesg output:
> hub.c: new USB device 00:1f.4-2, assigned address 5
> usb.c: USB device 5 (vend/prod 0x91e/0x3) is not claimed by any active driver.

Game over, right here.  Until you fix this there is no point to trying gpsd.

You need to reconfigure your Linux Kernel to support the GPS.  In this
case that means you need to compile the garmin driver in to the kernel.

I prefer to include these things directly in the kernel, but you may want
to try it as a module if that is your nromal practice.
