Stop your whining about slow mobile data prices and speeds!

Down under we're stuck with GPRS over GSM (around 40kB/s if you're lucky - 
i.e. sitting under a tower), and around 2c per kByte (depending on plan)! 
Then there's the CDMA 1xRTT offerings - somewhat faster, but still 
hideously expensive. Telstra (biggest telco here) has also rolled out EVDO 
'broadband' data - IF you're in one of the major capital cities, AND you 
buy the PCMCIA modem. And if you get out of the EVDO coverage (which is the 
rest of the country), it drops back to 1xRTT rates (and prices).

To cap it all off, the CDMA network is being decommissioned 'sometime 
soon'. I've been talking to manufacture's and suppliers of industrial 
wireless modems here, and Telstra still hasn't set a firm date for cut-off, 
or explained what technology is going to replace CDMA. There's plenty of 
motherhood statements about '3G' and 'Broadband for the Bush' - but nothing 
us techies need to get on with product planning and design work...

So think yourself lucky that you can get 110kbits for $10/month - I dream 
of being able to do that.

</more rant>
