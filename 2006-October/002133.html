Hi All,

Thanks for the software suggestions.

I'm a hardware engineer though - which is why I thought about building a 
serial sniffer....

A couple of jobs ago I had access to a HP protocol scanner (can't remember 
the number). This was a very nice (with a very nice HP price to go with it) 
bit of kit that would be slipped into the serial line between the devices 
in question. It could do autobauding, decode a number of different 
protocols running over RS232/RS422/RS485 and dump the whole lot onto the 
screen and to a log file. I got to know the thing intimately, as I spent 4 
months reverse engineering serial protocols for some equipment we acquired 
during a company buy-out....

You can also do software sniffing using realterm 
(http://realterm.sourceforge.com) and the appropriately hacked serial cable.

Another way to do it is to use a multi-port terminal server - device in on 
one port, and rebroadcast (via TCP) to other ports....and netcat on your pc.
