> one thing we did in the old (Win3.1) days to split our NMEA was to
> install 3-4 serial ports in the PC, have the incoming stream feed into
> com1, a small Qbasic or somesuch program then logged that stream &
> forwarded it to Tx on com2. A small serial cable connected com2 to com3
> & com3 fed the nav software. Round-about solution, but it worked pretty
> well and only took a few minutes to code.
> 

One way I did it in the very old DOS days, when I had to sniff on the
communication of a DOS application with a microcontroller over a serial
line was to change Dosemu in such a way that when I started it with a
certain error-reporting level it would write all communication over the
serial port to a logfile and then start the DOS application in Dosemu.
This worked very well with almost no time spend in programming and no
extra hardware. If a windows aplication will run in Wine it might be
possible to change Wine a little bit to snif on your communication.
