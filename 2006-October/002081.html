Massimo Burcheri wrote:
> I even had problems with serial lines longer then 5m. At this time I
> had to choose less than 1200 baud sometimes.
> I don't know how the industry deals with serial sensors for distances
> of more then 10 meters.

RS-232 is only rated for 25 feet or so. Yes, lowering the baud rate can
help. For longer distances you'll want to use RS-422. It's easy to find
a little self powered black box 232<->422 converter to plug in at either
end of the span. It wouldn't hurt to use well shielded thick cable &
match your twisted pairs as well.
