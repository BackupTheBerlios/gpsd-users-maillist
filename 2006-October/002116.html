Gary E. Miller <gem at rellim.com>:
> Conceptually should be easy to add in to gpsd.  It looks simple:
> 
> Digital Depth is embedded in the DPT sentence.  Its format is as follows:
> 
> INDPT,d.d,o.o*hh<CR><LF>
> 
> where:
> 
>     * d.d is the decimal depth value in meters relative to the transducer
>     * o.o is an offset from the the transducer to the surface.
>     * hh is an automatically generated checksum
>     * <CR><LF>
> 
> Eric would have to alocate some way for gpsd to present the info to the
> client.  Not a big deal, but just some bookeeping.  Eric: what do you
> think about this?

How is this different from altitude?  Is there any reason we shouldn't use the 
normal altitude fields(s)?


> > The timestamps that gpsd could add, for example, would provide
> > valuable position interpolation information for more precisely positioning
> > the depth readings between position readings.
> 
> gpsd has stayed away from interpolation of data.  The intent has been to
> just provide the facts to the client and let the client do any fancy
> processing of the data.
> 
> > Ideally, I would have the incoming sentences parsed on arrival at the
> > laptop, with position translated from lat-lon to UTM.
> 
> No one else has requested UTM.  GPS seem to be pretty much wgs84.  I
> guess it could be done, but seems to me to be the sort of thing best
> done in a client.  I would hate to see something like proj.4 linked in to
> gpsd.

Agreed.  gpsd's job is to give you clean data in a known reference frame.  How
you transform it afterward is a client-side issue.
