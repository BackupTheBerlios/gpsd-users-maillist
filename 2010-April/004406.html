The data stream (for me) is simple ASCI NMEA text.

If by "Rabbit" driver you mean a general purpose UDP/TCP driver, than I agree. Of course I wouldn't call it a "Rabbit" driver as one would want the functionality to be very general. In my case, I would not want gpsd to be changing the physical communication settings of the Rabbit and GPS on the fly. A switch from NMEA to binary would not be ok, for example, as many other subsystems use the raw NMEA strings from the GPS. That said, I could imagine other systems where this kind of reconfiguration of the GPS would be ok.

IMHO, the physical communication mechanism of communicating with the GPS (serial, USB, TCP/UDP) could stand alone from the communication itself, in the standard internet ethos (I've been reading Eric's books). I realize the devil is in the details and much of what gpsd does seems to be to configure the GPS for optimum operation, all of which makes this separation of message and messenger difficult. But gpsd should probably support the lowest common denominator - vanilla (if that exists) NMEA, input only at a minimum, over a whole host of communication methods. 

(my 2 cents)
 
-Val
