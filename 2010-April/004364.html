Hate to bring up the obvious, but is your firewall blocking you?

Also, how are you launching gpsd? In Ubuntu (and I assume Debian)
launching gpsd from /etc/init.d/gpsd feeds the contents
of /etc/default/gpsd to the daemon.

You must now feed -G to gpsd so that it will listen on interfaces other
than localhost.
