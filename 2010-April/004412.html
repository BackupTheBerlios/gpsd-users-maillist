And, in GPSD, it does.  It's already the case that some of our sensor
sources can be serial devices while others are sockets accepting data
from remote Ntrip and DGPSIP servers.

Supporting your case may be as simple as teaching GPSD to open a UDP
socket listening to whatever your emitter's IP address is, and then to
pass the datagrams straight to the packet sniffer as though they
came from yet another serial device.

If so, this would be half an hour of programming supposing I had a 
UDP-datagram source to test with.  Do you have a public address you
could broadcast these from?
