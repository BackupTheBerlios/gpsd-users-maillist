On Thu, Apr 22, 2010 at 6:30 AM, Val Schmidt <vschmidt at ccom.unh.edu> wrote:
> But gpsd should probably support the lowest common denominator - vanilla
> (if that exists) NMEA, input only at a minimum, over a whole host of
> communication methods.

GPSD does in fact support input-only. Take pretty much any
NMEA-speaking receiver, plug in with just Vcc, GND and TXD and you'll
have working gps. If the tty is read-only (maybe because your uid
isn't permitted to write) or writes are suppressed with the "-b" flag,
or communication to the receiver isn't connected, GPSD will deal just
fine.

It would be nice to use networked receivers too - either connecting to
a remote gpsd instance or one of these fancy expensive
ethernet-enabled devices. Trimble, Topcon, Ashtech, NovAtel and Javad
were the first few names that came to mind... anyone win a lottery
lately?

Backing up a couple of layers: can you enumerate "a whole host of
communication methods"? I can think of these:
- serial (and things that look like it: usbserial, bluetooth)
- usb that doesn't look like serial (garmin)
- ip connections. these look kind of like serial in that you read(2)
and write(2) on the socket/fd, but you don't have to deal with
termios(3) stuff.
- PCI devices. Poke a special register, get data back from a specially
mapped page...
- ???
