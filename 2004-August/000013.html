Please no, just report what the GPS says. In my navigation software the
trend arrow's direction is controlled by the heading and the size of the
arrow is controlled by speed. When you are stopped the arrow shrinks to
nothing.

[It is scaled so that the arrow points to where you are expected to be
in 20 minutes time. From there it is easy to eyeball from the arrow
where you will be in 5min or an hour too. I considered adding a
smoothing filter into the heading but in practice it worked remarkably
well & didn't need one.]



One useful proprietary Garmin message that might be worth mentioning is
PGRME for error estimation.

from nmeafaq.txt:
    $PGRME,15.0,M,45.0,M,25.0,M*22
       15.0,M       Estimated horizontal position error in metres (HPE)
       45.0,M       Estimated vertical error (VPE) in metres
       25.0,M       Overall spherical equivalent position error


e.g. for drawing confidence circles around your position
