My advice is to not wait on this. We don't know if we can fix it, we
don't have a proposed fix, so of necessity we don't have a way to test
the fix. I'm not even sure that it is within gpsd's remit to deal with
it.

Also, I don't think it is a major issue for anyone. If it is a major
issue, now would be a good time to say so.

You might note it in the release notes. Not every gpsd user is familiar
with the esoterica behind the issue.

    There is a rare glitch in how the GPS spacecraft report the
    date. You may see an incorrect date which is different from the
    current date by a multiple of approximately 19.6 years. The time
    of day (GMT) is correct. gpsd reports the time and date from the
    spacecraft with no attempt to correct the
    date. http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gps#Timekeeping_format
