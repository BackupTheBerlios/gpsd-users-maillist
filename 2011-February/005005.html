> What is "de-fang" udev ? I know where the udev/rules.d  files are, but  
> I don't know how to change them. I tried googling, but could not find  
> anything. Could you tell me how to put the gpsd to ignore a device  
> (/dev/ttyUBSx) or point me to where I can find it out ?

On my Ubuntu 10.04 box I would edit 
	/lib/udev/rules.d/40-gpsd.rules
and/or
	/lib/udev/gpsd.hotplug.wrapper
to try to detect your device and stop it being given to gpsd, e.g. run 
	lsusb
to find the vendor and device IDs for your serial port and comment out
the matching lines in /lib/udev/rules.d/40-gpsd.rules 

You may also need to edit
	/etc/default/gpsd
to set
	USBAUTO="false"
