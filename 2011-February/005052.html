On Wed, 23 Feb 2011, Damien R. wrote:

> I want to synchronize two or more machines with no internet connection and no
> wired or wireless connection between them.

That is impossible without SOME sort of connection.

[...]

> I have searched on the list and I read that I should use chrony instead of ntp
> for this purpose but it was not explain why.

ntpd is designed to be on a constant network connection with at least 3
other servers at all times.  It then uses a voting system to pick the
best candidate time.  Your setup does not meet this profile.

chrony is designed for intermittent updates, like for your setup.

> Is it possible to use ntp and gpsd for the described purpose ?

Probaby not. And why bother when there is a better solution.

> Are there better alternatives ?

Yes, chrony.

> Why does ntp discard the servers ?

Any of a number of reasons, check your ntpd logs and RTFM.
