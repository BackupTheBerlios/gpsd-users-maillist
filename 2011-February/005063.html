Yo Damien!

On Thu, 24 Feb 2011, Damien R wrote:

> So, to have the same time on both computer, I think I should sync each
> computer clock with a gps clock.
> But I do not know if it this approach will work. So my first question:
> > Is it possible to use ntp and gpsd for the described purpose ?

ntp stands for Network Time Protocol.  Since you have no net, you have
nothing to run ntp on!

> Is it appropriate to sync each computer with a gps (and so gpsd and ntp) to
> have the same time on each computer ?

Sort of. ntp (the protocol) will do you no good since you have no net.
But you can get good time from your GPS, and yes you can use ntpd (the
program, not the protocol) or chronyd to take the GPS time and apply it
to your local clock.

> And the second question was not about a ntp program but a more general
> question and maybe it is out of the scope of that list:
> Are there better alternatives than using gps to synchronize two or more
> computers ?

Got an atomic clock handy?  They can be had 'cheaply'.

> What is the best approach to have the same time (with microseconds
> resolution) on two or more computers (with no internet access, no connection
> between them) ?

Resolution is not the issue, you can easily get nSec resolution.
Stability and accuracy are the issues.  Do those nSec mean anything
(e.g. how closely do two hosts agree on the same time)?

There may be no 'best' solution.  Depending on your hardware it will be
easy to get two hosts to agree within 100uSec.  With good hardware and
careful tuning 10uSec is possible.  Only rarely do people report success
to 1uSec, and I doubt most of those reports.
