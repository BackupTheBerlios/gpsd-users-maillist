On Fri, 27 May 2011, Sebastian Arcus wrote:

> According to Wikipedia, it takes maximum 12.5 minutes to download a full
> almanac from a single satellite

First off, the almanac is a subset of the ephemeris, best not to confuse
them.

- From the IS-GPS-200E, Sections 20.3.3.5.1.2

"The almanac data are a reduced-precision subset of the clock and
ephemeris parameters.

With the almanac the GPS can determine the best sats to try, and then has
to download the current ephemeris for those sats for best accurary.

Wikipedia is wrong, a FULL almanac can take hours.  This is a very
commmon misconception.  In one 12.5 frame you will get ONE almanac set
containing an item for each sat.  GPS sats send several alamanac sets
for use at different times in the future.  Each set uses a different toa
(almanac reference time) and IODC (Issue of Data, Clock) so they can be
separated.  The current almanac set is transmitted frequently and the
long range sets (21 to 62 days) as infrequently as every 24 hours.  See
IS-GPS-200E, Table 20-XII.

So a FULL almanac can take 24 hours!

>  - so based on this information, I always
> assumed that, if I'm outdoors, without significant obstructions, and the
> dongle fails to obtain a lock in 12.5 minutes -

For a modern GPS.  Some older ones need to wait until the start of the cycle
and then take the full 12.5 mins.  I often see older Garmin take 30 mins
to get lock with a stale ephemeris.

There is a chicken/egg issue here.  A GPS needs the almanac to find a good
sat to lock on, but needs to lock on a sat to get the almanac!  So the
initial search may just be a brute force one and that takes time.

For a modern multiple channnel receiver the brute force approach works well.

> there must be something else
> wrong with the dongle or the chip is stuck somehow. Am I missing something in
> my logic?

You have identified the problem is mostly in the car.  When in the car, is the
antenna on the dash with a full sky view?  Or on the seat with a very bad
sky view?  Most GPS will not get any lock when on the passenger seat.
