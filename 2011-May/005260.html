I've actually just stumbled over the gpsmon program. I wonder what took 
me so long. This is more like it - some way of keeping an eye on the gps 
dongle and seeing a bit more closely what it is doing in realtime and 
how fast it is acquiring satellites - instead of staring with 
frustration waiting for the light on the dongle to start flashing.

Now for the slightly weird part. With gpsmon, I can actually get a fix 
from inside the house. Not only from inside the house (sometime I could 
get a fix upstairs) - I can actually get a fix downstairs! - with the 
roof, and a whole other floor above me - a full, proper fix, with 
flashing light on the dongle. This dongle is plane strange - yesterday 
couldn't get a fix in the middle of a field for 40 minutes - now I get a 
fix downstairs in the house, with a whole floor above me!

I suppose I'll just keep on testing it - but at least now I can use 
gpsmon to actually see if it is detecting any satellites and receiving 
any information - no more staring at blue lights wondering if the 
chipset is stuck in limbo.
