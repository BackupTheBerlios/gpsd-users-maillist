On Mon, 30 May 2011 10:43:40 +0100
Sebastian Arcus <shop at open-t.co.uk> wrote:

> I was just wondering why gpsd doesn't have a configuration file in 
> /etc/gpsd.conf, like most other Unix/Linux software?

Several reasons.

One of the minor reasons is that config files are PITA.

Having the software do it right the first time is a lot of work for the
developers but much less work for the end users. So the expertise is
required where it actually lies, with the developers, not the users.

I have given up on many programs not because they didn't work, but
because the documentation was terrible, and I couldn't get the program
to work. With its auto configuration, gpsd works right out of the box
most of the time, and users don't have to slog through the
documentation.

Probably the major reason is that the main customers for gpsd are not
us folks running around on the M4 with our Linux laptops and a GPS
receiver dongle. They are people building embedded applications, where
config files are not merely a PITA but a downright no-no.
