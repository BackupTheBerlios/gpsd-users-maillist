I have a similar setup, a Globalsat BU-353 (see
http://gpsd.berlios.de/hardware.html for some notes on it). It works
just fine once it gets a fix. Your times to first fix don't sound too
different from mine.

Another factor that affects time to first fix is the environment of the
receiver. Outdoors on a flat plain (Kansas, the mid-Pacific, etc) and
no cloud cover is ideal. Indoors the thing may never get a fix. I have a
steel roof and siding, which makes for a nice Faraday cage. Two meters
from the wall I may never get a fix. Other places to avoid are canyons,
especially urban canyons like midtown Manhattan. So your mileage will
definitely vary.
