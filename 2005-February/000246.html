James Cameron <quozl at us.netrek.org>:
> Why not have gpsd provide the data via pty so that any number of serial
> port demanding clients will work?  Why this fixation on TCP/IP?  ;-)

Um, because the world doesn't need three dozen different NMEA interpretation
engines?  It's not a fixation on TCP/IP that's at issue here, it's a fixation
on sealing away the appalling crud that NMEA 0183 has accumulated over decades
in just one place.  I'd feel the same way if the daemon's request channel
were shared-memory segments, named pipes, or smoke signals.

Half of the point of gpsd is that it's a multiplexing service.  The
other (less obvious but equally important half) is to engineer things
so that nobody else but me, thee, and the handful of other brave souls
on the GPSD dev list ever has to know about the murky depths of the
relevant standards.

When application writers let us concentrate on being the experts in
this odd little domain, application writers can concentrate on places
where *they* are experts.  Thus, everyone's felicity is increased.
