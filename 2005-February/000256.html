I am using Kismet-2005-01-R1 and gpsd 2.7. Just for grins and giggles, I did
a softlink 'ln -s /dev/ttyUSB0 /dev/gps0' and am calling gpsd with
'/usr/sbin/gpsd -n -p /dev/gps0'.

With my old Motorola PVT-6 receiver, kismet would consistently and
constantly lose gps signal, regardless of the GPS tweak setting inside
kismet.conf. Interestingly, if I went back to Kismet-2004-04-R1 and pre-1.9
gpsd, kismet was then happy as a clam.  Finally, I replaced my PVT-6 with a
Garmin GPS-16. Now kismet is running happier than ever, no lock loss
problems.

Best I can figure is/was that the PVT-6 was NMEA 1.0 and had very limited
sentence support. The GPS-16 is NMEA 2.0, and supports a hell of a lot more
sentences, plus the fact it's a 12-channel receiver and won't lose lock when
I drive under an overpass, which was a significant problem with the PVT-6.
