Once upon a time, Håkan Johansson <f96hajo at chalmers.se> said:
> So far, I have not been able to see any trace of the 1 us wide pulse 
> inside gpsd.  I've so far only tested with a USB TTL-RS232 cable, which 
> may have its own problems with such a short pulse.

I am running the PPS signal from my Trimble SVeeSix into the parallel
port (no serial port available), and IIRC it was also very short.  I
found that the port would not trigger on a pulse that narrow, so I used
a 555 timer (in one-shot mode) to get a wider pulse.  I had to also use
a 7404 inverter on the PPS signal before the 555, as the PPS output is
an active-high trigger, and the 555 looks for an active-low trigger
input.

This also means that my PPS signal gets a little more delay from the
inverter (~10ns) and the 555 (~100ns), but since that's well under the
claimed 1us accuracy (and who knows what additional delay is added by
the parallel port, CPU bus, etc.), I didn't worry about it.
