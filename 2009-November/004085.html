Yo Rich!

On Wed, 25 Nov 2009, Rich Wales wrote:

> > PPS works just fine on stock Linux kernels/distros.
> > Just add ntpd and gpsd.
>
> Do I not need to use the "LinuxPPS" kernel patches to make it work
> in Linux?

Correct.  You do not need to patch the kernel.  There may be advantages
to doing so, but not seen the need myself.

> I had been under the impression that PPS handling either wouldn't
> work at all, or would be very unreliable, in Linux without LinuxPPS.

Works for me, and a lot of others, YMMV.

> I'm not looking for nanosecond (or even microsecond) accuracy here,
> mind you.  Accuracy in the 10 - 100 microsecond range would be fine.

You are not going to get close to nanosecond with a GPS.  Most are
speced to 1uS.  Accuracy is pretty hard to prove and usually not
any where near what people think they have.  Just look at all the
stratum 1 servers that differ by 10mS or more.

Stability is a lot easier to show.  I have two different Linux hosts,
one getting PPS from a SiRF III and one from a Garmin GPS-18/LVC. ntpd
says they agree better than 10uS most of the time.  You will easily be
stable to less than 100uS.
