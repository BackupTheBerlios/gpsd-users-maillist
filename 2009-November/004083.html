Yo Rich!

On Wed, 25 Nov 2009, Rich Wales wrote:

> I'm using the current gpsd sources from SVN (revision 6600).

Good.  Been a lot of improvment lately.

> PPS_ENABLE is defined in gpsd_config.h, but TIOCMIWAIT appears to be a
> Linux-specific thing that doesn't exist in OpenBSD.

Yep, no TIOCMIWAIT then you get no PPS from gpsd.  Since I have no OpenBSD
I have no idea what the workaround might be.

On Linux the gps_ppsmonitor() uses ioctl(TIOCMIWAIT) to wait for a
change on the PPS pin.  That way it gets woken up promptly on a PPS
change and yet wastes no time in a wait loop.

I see two options, connect the PPS pin somehow directly to ntpd or
figure out a way to wait on the pin so gps_ppsmonitor() can work on
OpenBSD.

> I want to get this working in OpenBSD, if possible, because I'm
> looking for a software platform that will support PPS out of the box,
> without requiring a custom-built kernel -- and this appears to be a
> realistic possibility in OpenBSD, but not Linux or FreeBSD.

PPS works just fine on stock Linux kernels/distros.  Just add
ntpd and gpsd.
