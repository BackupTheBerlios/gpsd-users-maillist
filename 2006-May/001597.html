Yo Massimo!

gpsd ver 2.31 supports the -V command, but yours does not.  So you do
not appear to have gpsd 2.31 first in your path.  You have a pretty old
version of gpsd first in your path.

gpsdrive comes with its own old version of gpsd.  My guess is you have both
the gpsddrive version and the standlone version installed.

Try this, it takes a while and may throw some errors, but see what it
shows you about gpsd:

# find / -name gpsd -exec ls -lcd \;
