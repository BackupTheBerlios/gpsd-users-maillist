This gave me a great opportunity to get back on my SL.  And the bottom
line, your gpsd runs just find on my wrtsl54gs OpenWRT RC5.  I got the
pl2303 working and all.  I had to kludge (download without a package)
the stty from some web site.  stty -F /dev/usb/tts/0 4800

But, it looks like gpsd must take care of this after reboot.

Also, it didn't want to change my BU-303 from SIRF to NMEA so I went
back to sirfmon on my laptop.
sirfmon /dev/ttyUSB0 then press n to exit in NMEA.

I added this file to start gpsd after reboot.
> cat /etc/init.d/S50gpsd
#!/bin/sh
gpsd -p /dev/usb/tts/0 -s 4800 -P /var/run/gpsd.pid

But, 2.10 is so old it doesn't have the "o" command which is what I
based most of my Perl library (Net::GPSD) on.
