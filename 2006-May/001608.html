I'm not using gpsd with the GPS compass, but it is interfaced with 
Simrad branded Olex navigation software,
   http://www.olex.no/index_e_NY.html

I would be fairly surprised if Olex *wasn't* using Gpsd in two years
time (embedded linux).


Cap'n won't let me mess with the ship's navigation electronics (other
than to fix them when they go down ;), so for the science crew we run
dual GPS logging units (gpsd) without compass info. On another 250' ship
we use three internally-logging survey grade trimble units, one each at
either end of the forward bridge and one on the stern; but that's all
post-processed stuff.


Certainly course over ground != heading, and for a largish boat you need
to know where your bum is even when not under way.

I understand the DARPA Grand Challange folks were using similar GPS
compasses + gpsd?
