On Wednesday 03 May 2006 6:06am, Hamish wrote:
> > > Also, is there a gpsd query to get the current leap seconds?
>
> I am pretty sure it is sent to the GPS as part of the almanac, but I am
> not aware of a common NMEA message containing the info.

Yes, the "leap second" count is sent to the GPS receiver, but I believe that 
it's not in the almanac data (as I would have expected too) but rather in the 
continuous data stream, as part of the "UTC parameters".

In fact, some GPS receivers (e.g. my Garmin 'GPS35 TracPak') actually do send 
the leap second count. In my case, it's in the Garmin-proprietary (therefore, 
as Hamish said, not common) NMEA sentence known as '$PGRMF' ('GPS Fix Data', 
in the 5th field).
