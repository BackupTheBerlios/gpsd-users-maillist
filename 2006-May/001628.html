The receiver is measuring the phase difference between the arriving
signals. It's kind of like doing signal direction finding (DF) but
backwards.  Funny thing is that you need a satellite on the horizon to
get the best calculations.  I guess you only need two antennas because
you know where the satellites are. A terrestrial DF receiver needs at
least three antennas but typically uses four.  If you see a police car
with four antennas mounted on top in a square pattern that's a DF
capability for Lo-jack.
