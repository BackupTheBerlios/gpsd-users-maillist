That's not quite the way it works.  The leap-seconds don't delay when 
the information comes through: they just mean there will be a 
(slightly) different timestamp on the GPS message compared to a 
correct UTC time.  The speed that comes through "now" is still the 
speed you were doing "just a moment ago" (i.e. a second or so for the 
GPS to calculate speed from differentiating position against time, 
then to transmit a sentence for GPSd to pass on to cgps).

There are some bugs in speed unit handling yet to be patched in the 
repository, but those shouldn't affect cgps (although for the time 
being, don't trust the mode reported by cgps - that may not be the 
same as the mode the GPS itself is reporting).

In summary, the speed in cgps might be a second or two behind, but 
that's nothing to do with leap seconds.
