I have a TrueNorth unit and I am the one that added the code to gpsd. I 
did what seemed right at the time. As someone else mentioned this was 
incorporated as part of our Darpa Grange Challenge project. The original 
True North uses a capcitive fluid sensor to determine pitch and yaw. 
Because the fluid can slosh around when a vehicle is moving this doesn't 
work well above certain speeds. We wanted the compass to appear as a GPS 
unit providing heading information when our vehicle was moving slow, 
less than 2 mph slow. Otherwise the heading information from a GPS unit 
can start to wonder around and then it makes the vehicle look like it 
has a drunk driver behind the wheel.

Anyway, I have my True North compass and can setup it up and do some 
testing if people are interested. I am now incorporating it into a robot 
that I am building.

In the end, we did not employ gpsd in our vehicle. Instead I stripped 
the code down into very specific drivers for each NMEA device we used. I 
have a Trimble, Thales and True North specific driver that I used for 
our Darpa vehicle.
