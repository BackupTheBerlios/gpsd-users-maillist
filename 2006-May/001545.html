Yo Michael!

On Tue, 2 May 2006, Michael R. Davis wrote:

> Why is leap second compiled into gpsd?

Actually, it is not really about a "leap second".  This is for those
GPS too stupid to report the conversion from GPS time to UTC time.
The difference increments by one second every time a leap second is
proclaimed.  That is 14 seconds at the moment.  Most GPS do this
correctly without the help of gpsd.  The SiRF, Garmin, tsip and zodiac
drivers handle this dynamically.  Maybe others do too.

> I know we haven't had but one
> leap second in something like seven years.  But, it might be smart to
> have a default compiled in and a config file with the correct
> information

You planning on time traveling with your GPS?  If you really are time
traveling, how about getting a GPS that correctly reports the GPS to UTC
conversion, or properly reports UTC time and then this is superfulous.

> Also, is there a gpsd query to get the current leap seconds?

Not that I know of.  If your GPS is reporting this correctly then what
would you do with that info?  For the forseeable future it is a known
constant.
