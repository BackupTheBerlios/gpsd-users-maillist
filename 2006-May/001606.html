Ouch!  I don't have such a device, so I hadn't even looked at that 
code... but that is NOT a good way to handle it.  As anyone who 
navigates in or on fluids (i.e. in boats or aircraft) knows, it is 
only in very special circumstances that heading equals track, i.e.:

- if you happen to be on something solid
- if the air/water around you isn't moving, or
- if the air/water around you is only moving in exactly the same or 
exactly opposite direction as you are heading

For pilots and navigators, the difference between heading and track 
is essential information, just as the difference between airspeed and 
ground speed is essential.  To my knowledge there aren't generally 
available physical speed sensors (airspeed indicators or machmeters) 
that we should expect gpsd to handle, but we should definitely handle 
the True North device properly.

I'll draft a fix shortly, unless someone else beats me to it, but 
I'll have to let someone who has such a device test it.  The 
challenge is going to be coming up with an appropriate addition to 
the gpsd command set.  May discuss this further on the dev mailing 
list.
