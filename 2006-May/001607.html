Hi everyone,

There's currently discussion on the gpsd-dev list about the existing 
support for True North devices - specifically that the heading 
reported by such a device is handled by gpsd as a track, despite the 
fact that the two could be very different (as a pilot, I frequently 
encounter situations where heading and track differ by 10 degrees or 
more).

I've written a patch to make gpsd handle True North device input as 
heading, with a corresponding addition to the gpsd command suite 
(sending "h" in a query returns "H=[heading in degrees T]")... but 
before we can incorporate that into the daemon, we really need 
someone who has and uses such a device to test the patched gpsd in 
their application.  Even if you're not sure how to test such a patch, 
but you have and use such a device, please let me know as much detail 
about your situation as possible so that I can consider and deal with 
any potential consequences of this change.  Specifically:

- what hardware do you use (just a True North compass, or a True 
North compass plus one or more GPS units, or do you have a GPS unit 
which incorporates a magnetic heading sensor) ?
- what software do you use (presumably GPSd to connect to your 
hardware... but what client software for GPSd)?
- if you know, what kind of queries do you send to GPSd (Watcher 
mode, Raw mode, "O" queries, and/or specific queries such as "PAMVT")?

Thanks,
Robin
