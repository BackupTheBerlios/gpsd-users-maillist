On 19/11/2010 19:57, Gary E. Miller wrote:
>> it's a fact that the GPS Signal takes 12.5 minutes to
>> broadcast based on the size of the data format and the broadcast's bitrate.
>
> Your 'fact' is not a fact.  Maybe some, or most days, it is true, but it
> is not a requirement of the spec.  Maybe if gpsd can beef up its 50bps
> handling we can illuminate this a bit with real data.

Gary,

I don't see why not.

20.3.1 says: "The data stream shall be transmitted by the SV 
on the L1 and L2 channels at a rate of 50 bps."

20.3.2 says: "As shown in Figure 20-1, the message structure 
shall utilize a basic format of a 1500
bit long frame made up of five subframes, each subframe 
being 300 bits long. Subframes 4 and 5 shall be
subcommutated 25 times each, so that a complete data message 
shall require the transmission of 25 full frames."

This means the navigation message is 37500 bits (1500*25) 
long. At 50bps that is 750s, or 12.5 minutes.

This corroborates with the value that I have heard in the 
past, and that we see with the GPS quality monitoring 
product that we develop.

Am I missing something here?

Tom
