> After some searching I found a SiRFDemo tool from
> www.falcom.de/support/software-tools/sirf/
>
> SiRFDemo can set much more than Gpsinfo. However, the FAQ's say that
> the setting "are temporary until the receiver has exhausted both the
> internal Super-Cap".  What does this mean?
 
If you are to change any settings, like baud rate, Protocol,
enable/disable WAAS/EGNOS, or any other setting, these settings are
not permanent and will be erased once the device is away from power
for about 72 hours.

> How can the need of a cold-start or factory-reset be avoided: New
> firmware?
 
>From the information you provided below, you have the latest firmware.

> I use the receiver with gpsd http://gpsd.berlios.de/ This deamon
> cannot reset. What would be needed if a cold-start or factory-reset
> cannot be avoided? (the programming details).  

We usually recommend a cold start or factory reset to solve any issues
with GPS fix. If you continue to have these issues in a optimal
environment (like at a place with an open sky), then it could be that
your device is defective.

> I did some logging and saw that WAAS/EGNOS is disabled. How can I
> enable this?

You can enable WAAS/EGNOS using our GPSInfo application
(http://www.usglobalsat.com/downloads/GPSInfo.zip), Just check the box
that says "WAAS/EGNOS" and then perform a Cold Start, which will then
show you that WAAS is enabled. As I mentioned above, this is only
temporary and the BU-353 will reset to it's factory settings (WAAS
Disabled).
 
To Permanently have WAAS/EGNOS enabled, you'll to burn a different
firmware, which we can provide, but you'll need to perform the
firmware update at your own risk!
