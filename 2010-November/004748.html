> However, you've said that you are using the C interface and it's working
> well, why not stay with that?  I don't see why libgpsmm exists at all --
> it is such a thin wrapper over the C functions that there's really no
> difference other than an extra function-call on the stack and some
> wasted CPU cycles.

Agree with that. It doesn't use RAII - and makes funny bugs like closing an
unopened context possible. It returns a non-const pointer to the whole bloated
gps_data_t structure, or (even worse) NULL - and doesn't even try handling an
EAGAIN properly. To put it mildly, it doesn't look much like C++.
