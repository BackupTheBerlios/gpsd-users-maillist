Yo Tomalak!

On Fri, 19 Nov 2010, Tomalak Geret'kal wrote:

> 20.3.1 says: "The data stream shall be transmitted by the SV on the L1 and L2
> channels at a rate of 50 bps."
>
> 20.3.2 says: "As shown in Figure 20-1, the message structure shall utilize a
> basic format of a 1500
> bit long frame made up of five subframes, each subframe being 300 bits long.
> Subframes 4 and 5 shall be
> subcommutated 25 times each, so that a complete data message shall require the
> transmission of 25 full frames."
>
> This means the navigation message is 37500 bits (1500*25) long. At 50bps that
> is 750s, or 12.5 minutes.
>
> This corroborates with the value that I have heard in the past, and that we
> see with the GPS quality monitoring product that we develop.

That clears up the 12.5 mins thing.  That clearly describes the frames
that we see.  But I do not believe that every message is required to be
sent every frame.  Notice what 30.3.4.1 says:

"Broadcast system of messages is completely arbitrary, but sequenced
to provide optimum user performance. Message types 10 and 11 shall be
broadcast at least once every 48 seconds. All other messages shall be
broadcast in-between, not exceeding the maximum broadcast interval in
Table 30-XII. Message type 15 will be broadcast as needed, but will not
reduce the maximum broadcast interval of the other messages.  Type 15
messages that are longer than one page will not necessarily be broadcast
consecutively."

And as we previously saw, table 30-XII specficies some messages need
only be seen every 20min, 30min or 120min.
