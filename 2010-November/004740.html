Yo Pat!

On Tue, 9 Nov 2010, Pat Suwalski wrote:

> I'd ideally like gpsd to give me a fix in WGS84/UTS coordinates. From the
> documentation, I don't see a clean way of getting that information.

I assume that by UTS you meant UTM?

By default most every GPS is WGS84 (at least one of the many variants
thereof).  Best to just leave the GPS in WGS84 and convert after gpsd.

> Additionally, it seems that setting the Garmin GPS 18x USB receiver to UTS
> will be very difficult. The normal serial model works with their SNSRCFG
> program, where the datum and coordinate system can be set, but I can't get the
> USB product to be seen. I don't see anything in the documentation saying it
> won't work, but it doesn't.

The garmin USB protocol bears little relationship to the garmin serial
binary.  If something is not explicitly for garmin USB there is no way
it will work.

Even if you could force the GPS into UTM coordinates gpsd would barf on it.
