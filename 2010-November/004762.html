Yo Tomalek!

On Fri, 19 Nov 2010, Tomalak Geret'kal wrote:

> The almanac is in subframes 4-5 of the same signal (ephemeris is found in
> subframes 2-3), so the same scenario applies.

OK, you made me dig out the spec (IS-GPS-200D) and look it up.

According to Section 20.3.3.5.1.2

"The almanac data are a reduced-precision subset of the clock and
ephemeris parameters."

So when I said 'download the ephemeris', that would include the almanac, and
the complete ephemeris would be the larger and complete data set of the two.

> The 30 minute value is the minimum duration that the device was offline, not a
> figure to be counted after the device is restarted.

I'm looking at the Table 30-XII, Messsage Broadcast Intervals.

The 'Reduced Almanac' is broadcast at a minimum of every 20 mins and
the 'Midi Almanac' is only broadcast at a minimum of 120 mins.

The Differential Corrections are only broadcast every 30 mins.  So
potentially a dumb WAAS could have to wait up to an hour for WAAS to
activate.

While a smart GPS programmer can find ways to operate on partial data it
looks like lots of oportunities for a lazy programmer to just wait for
full data sets before computing position on a cold start.
