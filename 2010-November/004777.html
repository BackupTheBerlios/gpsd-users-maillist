> Hi Paolo,
>
> As the error message for your IPv4 binding indicates, you are already
> running an instance of gpsd.
>
> I see that you have tried to kill it, but you have tried to use some
> non-existent option of gpsd rather than the typical Linux command `killall`.
>
> Instead of `gpsd killall`, you ought to have written `killall gpsd`.
>
> If you perform `ps -al` to obtain a list of running processes, you should
> see that currently you have a gpsd instance running; after writing `killall
> gpsd` that should no longer be the case.
>
> You should then be able to start your new instance of gpsd.
>
> For further information, consult your favourite POSIX/UNIX manual on the
> `kill` and `killall` commands.
>
> Hope that helps,
