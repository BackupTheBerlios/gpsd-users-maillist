On Fri, 22 Jul 2005, Paulo Marcondes wrote:

>
> > Sorry, I was mistaken.  The 18 doesn't have a magnetic compass, and it
> > couldn't because it has a strong magnetic base for attaching to a car body.
>
> Most receivers don't. But you can always derive bearing information by
> doing simple geometry calculations from current and last positions.

The GPS 18 outputs a valid computed speed and direction.  No need to compute
that.  OTOH, you can not get the direction the GPS sensor is pointed at.

For example, the GPS my be traveling north, but actually be pointed south.
Or it may be stationary, but pointed east.

The only way to get the direction the GPS is pointed is with an internal
flux gate compass.  Sorta ike what new cars have in the read view mirror.

Some GPS for hiking have this.
