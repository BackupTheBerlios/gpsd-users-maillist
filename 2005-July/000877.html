On 7/17/05, Eric S. Raymond <esr at snark.thyrsus.com> wrote:
> I have backed the attempt to autoconfigure DBUS out of configure.ac
> following reports from Gary Miller, Rob Janssen, and Wolfgang
> Rupprecht that it broke their compiles.  You'll have to configure
> DBUS manually until somebody figures out how to tes for DBUS headers
> in a way that is distro-independent and DBUS-version-independent.

Attached is a fragment of some configure code I once wrote to go
searching for db-4.x. This has the same sort of problem: I've seen
/usr/{,local}/{include,lib}/{,db4} in the wild. What I chose to do was
to test for the logical places and then allow the user to specify
--with-db--{includes,libraries} if my guesses weren't good enough.

The configuration for mod_auth_kerb (modauthkerb.sf.net) does much the
same, but it checks for the environment variables KRB5_CPPFLAGS and
KRB5_LDFLAGS. Again, it tries to guess, and then provides a fairly
reasonable way to pass in compile-time kerberos information. This
makes sense, as kerberos is another one of those nightmare packages
that may be there, or might not be, and in all sorts of wierd paths.
