On 7/27/05, Wallace Owen <wally.owen at titan.com> wrote:
> It's likely that they don't think you care much about exact time of fix,
> but are more interested in exact time.  That's what the PPS signal is
> for, although it's only accurate to about 2uS, I _think_.

One microsecond. The spec for the PPS time message says:

"Output time associated with current 1 PPS pulse. Each message will be
output within a few hundred ms after the 1 PPS pulse is output and
will tell the time of the pulse that just occurred. The MID 52 will
report the UTC time of the 1 PPS pulse when it has a current status
message from the satellites. If it does not have a valid status
message, it will report time in GPS time, and will so indicate by
means of the status field."

And the hardware spec
(http://www.usglobalsat.com/item.asp?itemid=61&catid=16 is typical)
says "Time: 1us synchronized to GPS time"


GDB has a 'break' feature; why doesn't it have 'fix' too?
