>> One fear I have is that GPS's might tell us a peudorange but not tell
>> us the time that the observation was made with enough precision to be
>> of any use for calculated the correction.  We would need to know the
>> observation time down to a handful of nS.
>
> Yoicks.  From a GPS we're extremely unlikely to get better than milliseconds.

Pondering my SiRF_Binary_Protocol.pdf (p 3.32, msg 28) raises more
questions than it answers.  I am left to wonder why they tell us the
pseudorange down to meter resolution and time down to milliseconds.
The equivalent resolutions would be 1 meter = ~3nS.

Perhaps the answer is that they extrapolated the time to the next
round ms tick, but thats groping for straws.  Another answer might be
that they just expect us to work out the precise time from first
principles, but then again we need to have the assurance that the
pseudoranges are all calculated for the same exact time plus or minus
only a few nS.  I must be missing something obvious.
